**The Future Sound of London** (often abbreviated to FSOL) is a British [experimental](http://www.last.fm/tag/experimental) [electronic](http://www.last.fm/tag/electronic) music duo, formed in 1988 in Manchester, United Kingdom, and made up by Garry Cobain and Brian Dougans. Their music style covers most areas of electronic music, such as [techno](http://www.last.fm/tag/techno), [drum and bass](http://www.last.fm/tag/drum%20and%20bass), [house](http://www.last.fm/tag/house), [trip-hop](http://www.last.fm/tag/trip-hop), [ambient](http://www.last.fm/tag/ambient), [dub](http://www.last.fm/tag/dub), and often incorporates elements of [jazz](http://www.last.fm/tag/jazz), [classical](http://www.last.fm/tag/classical) and [psychedelic rock](http://www.last.fm/tag/psychedelic%20rock). In addition to music composition, they are involved in 2D and 3D computer graphics, video, animation (in making almost all their own videos for their singles), radio broadcasting and creating their own electronic devices for sound making. They are mostly known for the ambient-dub single “[Papua New Guinea](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Future+Sound+of+London/_/Papua+New+Guinea)” and the innovative ambient album _[Lifeforms](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Future+Sound+of+London/Lifeforms)_.       They have released works under numerous aliases like The Future Sound of London (main moniker), [Amorphous Androgynous](http://www.last.fm/music/Amorphous+Androgynous), [Yage](http://www.last.fm/music/Yage), [Humanoid](http://www.last.fm/music/Humanoid), [Amorphous Androgynous](http://www.last.fm/music/Amorphous+Androgynous), [Mental Cube](http://www.last.fm/music/Mental+Cube), [Q](http://www.last.fm/music/Q), [Zeebox](http://www.last.fm/music/Zeebox), [Heads of Agreement](http://www.last.fm/music/Heads+of+Agreement), [Semtex](http://www.last.fm/music/Semtex), [The Far-Out Son Of Lung](http://www.last.fm/music/+noredirect/The+Far-Out+Son+Of+Lung), [PART- SUB-MERGED](http://www.last.fm/music/PART-SUB-MERGED), [Art Science Technology](http://www.last.fm/music/Art+Science+Technology), and many more.      Garry Cobain and Brian Dougans met in the mid 80s while studying electronics at university in Manchester, England. Dougans had already been making electronic music for some time, working between Glasgow and Manchester, when they first began working in various local clubs. In 1988, Dougans embarked on a project for the Stakker graphics company. The result was the club hit “[Stakker Humanoid](http://www.last.fm/music/+noredirect/Future+Sound+Of+Londo n/_/Stakker+Humanoid)”, issued under [Humanoid](http://www.last.fm/music/Humanoid) alias. In the following three years the pair produced music under a variety of aliases, releasing a plethora of singles and EPs, including the successful bleep techno singles “Q” and “Metropolis”.
