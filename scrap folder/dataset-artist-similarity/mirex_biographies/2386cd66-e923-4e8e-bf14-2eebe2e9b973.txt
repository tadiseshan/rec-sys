**[3 Doors Down](http://www.3doorsdown.com/)** is a [post-grunge](http://www.last.fm/tag/post-grunge) influenced [alternative rock](http://www.last.fm/tag/alternative%20rock) group from Escatawpa, Mississippi, United States that formed in 1996. Consisting of Brad Arnold (vocals), Chet Roberts (guitar, a replacement for Matt Roberts), Todd Harrell (bass), Chris Henderson (guitar), and Greg Upchurch (drums), the band is known for several successful [rock](http://www.last.fm/tag/rock) singles such as “[Here Without You](http://www.last.fm/music/3+Doors+Down/_/Here+Without+You)” and “[Kryptonite](http://www.last.fm/music/3+Doors+Down/_/Kryptonite)”. The group signed to Universal Records in 2000.      Universal then released their first album, ‘_[The Better Life](http://www.last.fm/music/3+Doors+Down/The+Better+Life)_’, and they garnered a big fan response. They received international fame with their first single, “[Kryptonite](http://www.last.fm/music/3+Doors+Down/_/Kryptonite)”, in particular. The album went on to sell over 6 million copies.      In 2002, ‘_[Away from the Sun](http://www.last.fm/music/3+Doors+Down/Away+from+the+Sun)_’ was released with the single “[When I’m Gone](http://www.last.fm/music/3+Doors+Down/_/When+I%27m+Gone)”. The ballad “[Here Without You](http://www.last.fm/music/3+Doors+Down/_/Here+Without+You)” became another multi-format success, doing especially well in the adult contemporary space, while the singles “[The Road I’m On](http://www.last.fm/music/3+Doors+Down/_/The+Road+I%27m+On)” and “[Away from the Sun](http://www.last.fm/music/3+Doors+Down/_/Away+from+the+Sun)” were minor hits. The album went on to sell over 3 million copies.      3 Doors Down released their third studio album, [Seventeen Days](http://www.last.fm/music/3+Doors+Down/Seventeen+Days), in 2005, and it debuted at #1 on the Billboard Music Chart. The album achieved platinum status in less than a month. Its first single, “[Let Me Go](http://www.last.fm/music/3+Doors+Down/_/Let+Me+Go)”, received much airplay, and was followed by a second song called “[Behind Those Eyes](http://www.last.fm/music/3+Doors+Down/_/Behind+Those+Eyes).”      In 2007, they made a music video with the upcoming track “Citizen Soldiers” for the National Guard.      In May of 2008 3 Doors Down released their 4th full length studio album the self titled “3 Doors Down”. The first single is [It’s Not My Time](http://www.last.fm/music/3+Doors+Down/_/It%27s+Not+My+Time). They also launched a world tour in March in support.      Early years (1996-2000)      3 Doors Down was formed by drummer vocalist Brad Arnold, guitarist Matt Roberts and bassist Todd Harrell. The three were friends who had grown up together in Escatawpa, and who all shared a common interest in rock music. The band began playing locally around the city, playing a mix of both cover songs and original songs that would eventually be recorded on their first release
