**Beastie Boys** were an American [hip-hop](http://www.last.fm/tag/hip-hop) trio from New York City, United States: Brooklyn and Manhattan. The members were [Mike D](http://www.last.fm/music/Mike+D) (real name Michael Diamond), [Ad-Rock](http://www.last.fm/music/Ad-Rock) (Adam Horovitz) and, until his death in May 2012, [MCA](http://www.last.fm/music/MCA) (Adam Yauch).      The Beastie Boys were the first successful white rap group and one of the few acts from the early days of hip-hop that still enjoyed major success their whole career Their rock and punk-influenced rap has had a significant impact on artists both in and outside the hip-hop scene, and they were the first rap group to gain a substantial following with [alternative rock](http://www.last.fm/tag/alternative%20rock) fans.      The Beastie Boys came together in 1979 as a punk band called The Young Aboriginies. In 1981 MCA joined the group and from the suggestion of their guitarist John Berry, they changed their name to Beastie Boys. Their line up then consisted of Adam Yauch (aka MCA) on bass, drummer Kate Schellenbach (later of [Luscious Jackson](http://www.last.fm/music/Luscious+Jackson)), guitarist John Berry (of Big Fat Love), and Mike Diamond (aka Mike D) on the mic. Beastie Boys’ debut EP, the Pollywog Stew vinyl 7” was released in 1982.      The band’s first foray into hip hop, the Cooky Puss 12”, followed in 1983, with [The Young and the Useless](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Young+and+the+Useless) guitarist Adam Horovitz (aka Adrock) replacing John Berry. “Cooky Puss” would be the first B Boys record to receive play at NYC clubs like Danceteria as the band played its first shows outside the city.      The Mike D/MCA/Adrock Beastie Boys lineup debuted in 1984 with the “Rock Hard”/”Beastie Groove” 12.” Produced by [Rick Rubin](http://www.last.fm/music/Rick+Rubin), who went on to produce albums for the [Red Hot Chili Peppers](http://www.last.fm/music/Red+Hot+Chili+Peppers), [Johnny Cash](http://www.last.fm/music/Johnny+Cash) and [Audioslave](http://www.last.fm/music/Audioslave). The “She’s On It”/ “Slow And Low” 12” followed in 1985 and Beastie Boys went on Madonna’s “Virgin Tour.” as her opening act.      [Licensed to Ill](http://www.last.fm/music/Beastie+Boys/Licensed+to+Ill) dropped in fall 1986 and became the first Beastie Boys album—and the first Rap album ever—to go #1. Fueled by [Fight for Your Right](http://www.last.fm/music/Beastie+Boys/_/Fight+for+Your+Right) and [No Sleep Till Brooklyn](http://www.last.fm/music/Beastie+Boys/_/No+Sleep+Till+Brooklyn) it remained at #1 for seven weeks and simultaneously reached #2 on the urban chart, becoming the fastest selling debut to date for Columbia and the first hip hop record to break 5 million.      In 1989 Beastie Boys released their second album Paul’s Boutique produced by Beastie Boys and the Dust Brothers
