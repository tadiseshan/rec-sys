**Kevin Ayers** was a British songwriter, musician from Kent, United Kingdom, who was one of a group of [avant garde](http://www.last.fm/tag/avant%20garde) musicians who came together in Canterbury in the 1960’s and developed a whole new approach to the [psychedelic](http://www.last.fm/tag/psychedelic), [progressive](http://www.last.fm/tag/progressive) rock genre, known as the [canterbury scene](http://www.last.fm/tag/canterbury%20scene). His early work included works with artists like [Daevid Allen](http://www.last.fm/music/Daevid+Allen), [David Bedford](http://www.last.fm/music/David+Bedford) and [Mike Ratledge](http://www.last.fm/music/Mike+Ratledge). Since then Kevin Ayers wrote and recorded some of the wittiest and most poignant songs imaginable and produced more banana related lyrics than anyone ever dreamed possible.      The first serious band was [The Wilde Flowers](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Wilde+Flowers) 1964-1967) with [Hugh Hopper](http://www.last.fm/music/Hugh+Hopper), [Robert Wyatt](http://www.last.fm/music/Robert+Wyatt) and [Richard Sinclair](http://www.last.fm/music/Richard+Sinclair). This early hippie group transformed into [Soft Machine](http://www.last.fm/music/Soft+Machine). While the Softs went on, others split to form [Caravan](http://www.last.fm/music/Caravan) and [Gong](http://www.last.fm/music/Gong). Ayers put together his own band [Kevin Ayers & The Whole World](http://www.last.fm/music/Kevin+Ayers+&+The+Whole+World) featuring [Lol Coxhill](http://www.last.fm/music/Lol+Coxhill), [David Bedford](http://www.last.fm/music/David+Bedford) and a very young [Mike Oldfield](http://www.last.fm/music/Mike+Oldfield).      Ayers’ career was littered with great music and fine collaborations with more fine artists like [Danny Thompson](http://www.last.fm/music/Danny+Thompson), [John Cale](http://www.last.fm/music/John+Cale), [Nico](http://www.last.fm/music/Nico), [Brian Eno](http://www.last.fm/music/Brian+Eno), [Ollie Halsall](http://www.last.fm/music/Ollie+Halsall) and [wizards of twiddly](http://www.last.fm/music/wizards+of+twiddly).
