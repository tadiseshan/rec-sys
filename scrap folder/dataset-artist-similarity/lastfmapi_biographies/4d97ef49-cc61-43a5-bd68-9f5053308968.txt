Vincent Delerm (born August 31, 1976 in Évreux, [France](http://www.last.fm/place/France)) is a French singer-songwriter, pianist and composer. He is the son of the writer [Philippe Delerm](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philippe_Delerm).      His [self-titled debut](http://www.last.fm/music/Vincent+Delerm/Vincent+Delerm) was released in April 2002, and received praise from critics for its [intimist](http://www.last.fm/tag/intimist) moods. The song [Tes parents](http://www.last.fm/music/Vincent+Delerm/_/Tes+parents) received notable attention. Delerm’s second album, [Kensington Square](http://www.last.fm/music/Vincent+Delerm/Kensington+Square), was released in April 2004, and a third, [Les Piqûres d’araignée](http://www.last. fm/music/Vincent+Delerm/Les+Piq%C3%BBres+d%27araign%C3%A9e), in September 2006. All three albums were released on the record label [Tôt Ou Tard](http://www.last.fm/label/T%C3%B4t+Ou+Tard).
