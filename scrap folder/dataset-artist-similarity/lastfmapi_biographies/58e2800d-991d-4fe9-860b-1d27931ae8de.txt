British synth player, born December 10, 1958. Enjoyed success on the [underground](http://www.last.fm/tag/underground) [dance](http://www.last.fm/tag/dance) scene in the early 80’s before breaking into the mainstream with “[19](http://www.last.fm/music/Paul+Hardcastle/_/19)”, an international smash which spent five weeks at number one in the UK. In the latter half of the 1980s he specialised in [tv soundtrack](http://www.last.fm/tag/tv%20soundtrack) work. He made the theme tunes for Top of The Pops and Saturday Live, popular British entertainment shows. Paul now records mainly under the pseudonym [Jazzmasters](http://www.last.fm/music/Jazzmasters).      Early tracks of note include the popular ‘[Rain Forest](http://www.last.fm/music/Paul+Hardcastle/_/Rain+Forest)’ and ‘[King Tut](http://www.last.fm/music/Paul+Hardcastle/_/King+Tut)’, both were big dance hits in the U.S.      Paul was also half of the pop-funk duo [Kiss the Sky](http://www.last.fm/music/Kiss+the+Sky).      Paul has also remixed countless artists including ‘[D-Train](http://www.last.fm/music/+noredirect/D-Train)’, ‘[Third World](http://www.last.fm/music/Third+World)’, ‘[Barry White](http://www.last.fm/music/Barry+White)’, ‘[Five Star](http://www.last.fm/music/Five+Star)’ to name but a few!
