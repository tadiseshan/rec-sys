Laurel Aitken (April 22, 1927-July 17, 2005) became famous as one of the originators of [jamaica](http://www.last.fm/tag/jamaica)n [ska](http://www.last.fm/tag/ska) music in the late 1950s.      Aitken was born as Lorenzo Aitken in [cuba](http://www.last.fm/tag/cuba), and is the brother of singer [Bobby Aitken](http://www.last.fm/music/Bobby+Aitken). He moved to England in the 1960s, and had a loyal following among Skinheads and other ska fans. He had massive hits in the 1950s through 1970s, while recording for [Blue Beat Records](http://www.last.fm/label/Blue+Beat+Records), [Pama Records](http://www.last.fm/label/Pama+Records) and [Trojan Records](http://www.last.fm/label/Trojan+Records). He recorded one of the earliest ska records, the double A-sided [Boogie In My Bones](http://www.last.fm/music/Laurel+Aitken/_/Boogie+In+My+Bones) / [Little Sheila](http://www.last.fm/music/Laurel+Aitken/_/Little+Sheila) (1958). It was the first record on producer Chris Blackwell’s [Island Records](http://www.last.fm/label/Island+Records) label.      Aitken, who is known as “The Godfather of Ska”, made his home in Leicester UK and performed live almost until his death. He suffered from a serious illness following a heart attack about a year before he died. He died from a heart attack in [leicester](http://www.last.fm/tag/leicester), [uk](http://www.last.fm/tag/uk) on July 17, 2005.
