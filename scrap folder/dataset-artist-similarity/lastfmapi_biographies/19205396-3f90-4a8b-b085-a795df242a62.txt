Eliza Carthy (b. 1975) is an English folk singer and violinist.      Born on 23rd August 1975, she is the daughter of legendary English folk musicians singer/guitarist [Martin Carthy](http://www.last.fm/music/Martin+Carthy) and singer [Norma Waterson](http://www.last.fm/music/Norma+Waterson). She formed [The Waterdaughters](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Waterdaughters) with her mother, aunt (Lal Waterson), and cousin Maria Knight in 1989. She has subsequently worked with Nancy Kerr, and also with her parents as [Waterson:Carthy](http://www.last.fm/music/Waterson:Carthy), in addition to her own solo work.      She has twice been nominated for the Mercury Music Prize for UK album of the year: in 1998 for Red Rice, and again in 2003 for Anglicana. In 2003 Carthy swept the boards at the BBC Radio 2 Folk Awards, winning Folk Singer of the Year, Best Album (for _Anglicana_), and Best Traditional Track (for “Worcester City”).      In 2004 she was part of [Oysterband](http://www.last.fm/music/Oysterband)’s Big Session, a collaboration with numerous folk artists. They produced an album _The Big Session Volume One_, and the group as a whole were awarded Best Group at the Folk Awards in 2005.
