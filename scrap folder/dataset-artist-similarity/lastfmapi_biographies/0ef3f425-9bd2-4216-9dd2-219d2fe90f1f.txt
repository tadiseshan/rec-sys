**Leonard Albert “Lenny” Kravitz** (born May 26, 1964. New York, USA) is an [american](http://www.last.fm/tag/american) [singer-songwriter](http://www.last.fm/tag/singer-songwriter), record [producer](http://www.last.fm/tag/producer), [guitarist](http://www.last.fm/tag/guitarist) and actor whose [retro](http://www.last.fm/tag/retro) amalgam of [rock](http://www.last.fm/tag/rock), [pop](http://www.last.fm/tag/pop), [funk](http://www.last.fm/tag/funk), and even [techno](http://www.last.fm/tag/techno) is inspired by such music icons as [David Bowie](http://www.last.fm/music/David+Bowie), [Jimi Hendrix](http://www.last.fm/music/Jimi+Hendrix) and [John Lennon](http://www.last.fm/music/John+Lennon). Like [Prince](http://www.last.fm/music/Prince) and [Sly Stone](http://www.last.fm/music/Sly+Stone) before him, Kravitz uses a multi-ethnic, mixed-gender backing band.      Kravitz plays guitar, bass, drums, keyboards, percussion, and sitar. He often plays these instruments on each recorded track, but frequently collaborates with other musicians as well when recording.      His 1989 debut album _[Let Love Rule](http://www.last.fm/music/Lenny+Kravitz/Let+Love+Rule)_ was a moderate success. In 1991 he released his second album, _[Mama Said](http://www.last.fm/music/Lenny+Kravitz/Mama+Said)_, which reached the top 40 of the Billboard album charts. In 1993, the album _[Are You Gonna Go My Way](http://www.last.fm/music/Lenny+Kravitz/Are+You+Gonna+Go+My+Way)_ was released, reaching #12 on the Billboard 200 and Kravitz earned a Brit Award for best international male artist in 1994. He released the _[Circus](http://www.last.fm/music/Lenny+Kravitz/Circus)_ album in 1995, which reached number 10 on the Billboard chart on the back of his past achievement. With _[5](http://www.last.fm/music/Lenny+Kravitz/5)_ (1999), Kravitz embraced digital technology such as Pro Tools and samplers for the first time. He would win the first of his four consecutive Grammy for Best Male Rock Vocal Performance at the Grammy Awards of 1999.      His cover version of [The Guess Who](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Guess+Who)’s hit “_[American Woman](http://www.last.fm/music/Lenny+Kravitz/_/American+Woman)_” won him another Grammy for Best Male Rock Vocal Performance at the Grammy Awards of 2000. Kravitz released a _[Greatest Hits](http://www.last.fm/music/Lenny+Kravitz/Greatest+Hits)_ compilation in 2000. It proved to be his most successful album. The single “_[Again](http://www.last.fm/music/Lenny+Kravitz/_/Again)_” would earn him his third consecutive Grammy for the Best Male Rock Vocal in the Grammy Awards of 2001. Kravitz released his sixth album _[Lenny](http://www.last.fm/music/Lenny+Kravitz/Lenny)_ in October 2001. He won his fourth consecutive Grammy for Best Male Rock Vocal at the Grammy Awards of 2002.
