**Janis Joplin** (born 19 January 1943 in Port Arthur, Texas, United States, died 4 October 1970 in Los Angeles, California) was an American singer, songwriter, composer and painter. Originally the lead singer for the [blues rock](http://www.last.fm/tag/blues%20rock) band [Big Brother & The Holding Company](http://www.last.fm/music/Big+Brother+&+The+Holding+Company), Joplin left the band in late 1968 for a solo career. She released two solo albums, _[I Got Dem Ol’ Kozmic Blues Again Mama!](http://www.last.fm/music/Janis+Joplin/I+Got+Dem+Ol%27+Kozmic+Blues+Again+Mama!)_ (1969) and _[Pearl](http://www.last.fm/music/Janis+Joplin/Pearl)_ (1971), the latter released after her death. During her solo career, she was backed by the [Kozmic Blues Band](http://www.last.fm/music/Kozmic+Blues+Band) and later, the [Full Tilt Boogie Band](http://www.last.fm/music/Full+Tilt+Boogie+Band). Her version of the [Kris Kristofferson](http://www.last.fm/music/Kris+Kristofferson) song “[Me and Bobby McGee](http://www.last.fm/music/Janis+Joplin/_/Me+and+Bobby+McGee)” reached #1 in the United States on March 20, 1971.
