**Chris Botti** (born October 12, 1962 in Portland, Oregon, USA) is a [smooth jazz](http://www.last.fm/tag/smooth%20jazz) [trumpet](http://www.last.fm/tag/trumpet)er. His famous [muted trumpet](http://www.last.fm/tag/muted%20trumpet) is the same model that was once used by [Miles Davis](http://www.last.fm/music/Miles+Davis). He plays a Martin Committee trumpet made 1940 Handicraft and a mouthpiece from Bach made in 1921 Mt Vincent Bach Corp 3C. He has performed with artists such as [Sting](http://www.last.fm/music/Sting), [Paul Simon](http://www.last.fm/music/Paul+Simon), [Josh Groban](http://www.last.fm/music/Josh+Groban), [Dave Koz](http://www.last.fm/music/Dave+Koz), [Jeff Lorber](http://www.last.fm/music/Jeff+Lorber), [Jill Scott](http://www.last.fm/music/Jill+Scott), [Michael Bublé](http://www.last.fm/music/Michael+Bubl%C3%A9), [John Mayer](http://www.last.fm/music/John+Mayer), and [Brian Culbertson](http://www.last.fm/music/Brian+Culbertson).      Botti is also a former radio host. His show, called “Chill with Chris Botti,” focused mainly on “[chill](http://www.last.fm/tag/chill)” music, which is described as “[smooth jazz](http://www.last.fm/tag/smooth%20jazz) with a touch of [cool jazz](http://www.last.fm/tag/cool%20jazz).”
