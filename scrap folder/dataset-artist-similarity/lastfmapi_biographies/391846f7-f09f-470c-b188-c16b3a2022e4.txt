Alexandre Michel Gérard Desplat (born 23 August 1961 in Paris, France) is a French film score composer. Notably, he has received six Academy Award nominations, six BAFTA nominations, five Golden Globe nominations, five Grammy nominations and six César nominations, winning a Golden Globe for his work on “[The Painted Veil](http://www.last.fm/music/Alexandre+Desplat/The+Painted+Veil)” (2006), a Grammy for his work on “[The King’s Speech](http://www.last.fm/music/Alexandre+Desplat/The+King%27s+Speech)” (2011) and a César for his work on “[De Battre Mon Cœur S’est Arrêté](http://w ww.last.fm/music/Alexandre+Desplat/De+Battre+Mon+C%C5%93ur+S%27est+Arr%C3%AAt% C3%A9)” (2006) and “[The Ghost Writer](http://www.last.fm/music/Alexandre+Desplat/The+Ghost+Writer)” (2011). In 2011, Desplat won his first British Academy Film Award for his score for “[The King’s Speech](http://www.last.fm/music/Alexandre+Desplat/The+King%27s+Speech)”.      Desplat was born in Paris to a French father and a Greek mother. At the age of five, he began playing piano. He also became proficient on trumpet and flute. He studied with [Claude Ballif](http://www.last.fm/music/Claude+Ballif) and [Iannis Xenakis](http://www.last.fm/music/Iannis+Xenakis) in France and [Jack Hayes](http://www.last.fm/music/Jack+Hayes) in the U.S. Desplat’s musical interests were wide, and he was also influenced by South American and African artists and teachers, among whom were [Carlinhos Brown](http://www.last.fm/music/Carlinhos+Brown) and [Ray Lema](http://www.last.fm/music/Ray+Lema).      Desplat has composed extensively for films, in France and in Hollywood - scores and incidental music for over 100 films including “Lapse of Memory” (1992), “Family Express” (1992), “[Regarde les hommes tomber](http://www.last.fm/music/Alexandre+Desplat/Regarde+les+hommes+tomber)” (1994), “[Les Péchés Mortels](http://www.last.fm/music/Alexandre+Desplat/Les+P %C3%A9ch%C3%A9s+Mortels)” (1995), César-nominated “[Un héros très discret](htt p://www.last.fm/music/Alexandre+Desplat/Un+h%C3%A9ros+tr%C3%A8s+discret)” (1996), “Une Minute de Silence” (1998), “Sweet Revenge” (1998), “[Le Château des Singes](http://www.last.fm/music/Alexandre+Desplat/Le+Ch%C3%A2teau+des+Sin ges)” (1999), “Home Sweet Home” (2001), “[Reines D’Un Jour](http://www.last.fm/music/Alexandre+Desplat/Reines+D%27Un+Jour)” (2001), the César-nominated “[Sur Mes Lèvres](http://www.last.fm/music/Alexandre+Desplat/Sur+Mes+L%C3%A8vres)” (2002), “[Rire et Châtiment](http://www.last.fm/music/Alexandre+Desplat/Rire+et+Ch%C3%A2timent)” (2003), “[Fantastic Mr. Fox](http://www.last.fm/music/Alexandre+Desplat/Fantastic+Mr.+Fox)” (2009) and “[Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows: Part 1](http://www.last.fm/music/Alexa ndre+Desplat/Harry+Potter+and+the+Deathly+Hallows:+Part+1)” (2010).
