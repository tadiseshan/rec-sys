Mindy Smith (born June 1, 1972 on Long Island, New York) is an American singer-songwriter. Mindy rose to fame in 2003 when she was the first artist signed to contribute to the [Dolly Parton](http://www.last.fm/music/Dolly+Parton) tribute album _Just Because I’m a Woman: Songs of Dolly Parton_, on which she sang a cover of Parton’s classic “Jolene”. Later [Dolly Parton](http://www.last.fm/music/Dolly+Parton) followed by adding backing vocals to a new mix of the song, which was then featured on Smith’s debut album that was released in January 2004, [One Moment More](http://www.last.fm/music/Mindy+Smith/One+Moment+More).      In 2006 she released her second album [Long Island Shores](http://www.last.fm/music/Mindy+Smith/Long+Island+Shores). Smith included [Buddy Miller](http://www.last.fm/music/Buddy+Miller) as a duet vocalist for [What If The World Stops Turning?](http://www.last.fm/music/Mindy +Smith/_/What+If+The+World+Stops+Turning%3F)      In October 2007, Smith released a Christmas album, [My Holiday](http://www.last.fm/music/Mindy+Smith/My+Holiday). In addition to several standards, Smith wrote six original Christmas-themed songs. One standout, [I Know The Reason](http://www.last.fm/music/Mindy+Smith/_/I+Know+The+Reason), includes duet vocals by [Thad Cockrell](http://www.last.fm/music/Thad+Cockrell).
