Talib Kweli Greene (born October 3, 1975 in Brooklyn, New York) is an American MC known as one of the most prominent rappers in underground hip-hop, and is critically acclaimed frequently, despite not being commercially successful. Kweli first gained recognition through [Black Star](http://www.last.fm/music/Black+Star), a collaboration with fellow MC [Mos Def](http://www.last.fm/music/Mos+Def). He released his solo debut album Quality in 2002 and has since released 2004’s The Beautiful Struggle, 2005’s mixtape album Right About Now, 2007’s Eardrum, 2011’s Gutter Rainbows and 2013’s Prisoner of Conscious. He has also released two albums with [Hi- Tek](http://www.last.fm/music/Hi-Tek) under the name [Reflection Eternal](http://www.last.fm/music/Reflection+Eternal) and a collaboration album with [Madlib](http://www.last.fm/music/Madlib) in 2007 plus two albums with [Res](http://www.last.fm/music/Res) under the name [Idle Warship](http://www.last.fm/music/Idle+Warship).      His name is Arabic, meaning ‘the seeker’ or ‘student of truth and knowledge’. His parents were both college professors: his mother an English professor, his father a sociology professor. As a youth, he was drawn to Afrocentric rappers, such as [De La Soul](http://www.last.fm/music/De+La+Soul) and other members of the [Native Tongues Posse](http://www.last.fm/music/Native+Tongues+Posse) and in 1995 began recording with producer [Hi-Tek](http://www.last.fm/music/Hi- Tek) and rapper [Mos Def](http://www.last.fm/music/Mos+Def) who he met at central park, and later attended New York University with. With Mos Def (together the pair were known as [Black Star](http://www.last.fm/music/Black+Star)), Kweli achieved some mainstream success with Mos Def & Talib Kweli Are Black Star (1998). Also noted are Kweli and Mos’ central input on Rawkus Records’ compilation album Soundbombing (1997). Soundbombing also include collaborations between Kweli and DJ Hi Tek as [Reflection Eternal](http://www.last.fm/music/Reflection+Eternal). Together they released [Train of Thought](http://www.last.fm/music/Reflection+Eternal/Train+of+Thought) in 2000, which sold better than most alternative hip hop albums at the time. He has since released a critically acclaimed solo debut, [Quality](http://www.last.fm/music/Talib+Kweli/Quality) in 2002. In 2004, he released his second solo album, [The Beautiful Struggle](http://www.last.fm/music/Talib+Kweli/The+Beautiful+Struggle). His third solo album [Ear Drum](http://www.last.fm/music/Talib+Kweli/Ear+Drum) was released on August 21st, 2007.      Kweli has created his own record label, Blacksmith Records and has recently signed acclaimed female rapper [Jean Grae](http://www.last.fm/music/Jean+Grae), with rumors continuing to grow that underground veteran [MF DOOM](http://www.last.fm/music/MF+DOOM) is the next artist to be picked up. He left Geffen Records and has signed with the Warner Music Group
