Allen Toussaint (born January 14, 1938) is an American musician, songwriter and record producer and one of the most influential figures in New Orleans R&B who was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 1998. In the 1960s and 1970s, he was a prolific writer, producer and arranger, creating numerous hits for a variety of artists including [Lee Dorsey](http://www.last.fm/music/Lee+Dorsey), [Robert Palmer](http://www.last.fm/music/Robert+Palmer), [The Showmen](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Showmen), [The Meters](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Meters), [Betty Wright](http://www.last.fm/music/Betty+Wright), [Solomon Burke](http://www.last.fm/music/Solomon+Burke) and [Willie West](http://www.last.fm/music/Willie+West). He produced material for [Dr John](http://www.last.fm/music/+noredirect/Dr+John), [LaBelle](http://www.last.fm/music/LaBelle), [Chocolate Milk](http://www.last.fm/music/Chocolate+Milk) and [Aaron Neville](http://www.last.fm/music/Aaron+Neville) amongst many more.      An accomplished pianist and lyricist, Toussaint wrote many hit songs including early instrumental efforts [Java](http://www.last.fm/music/Al+Hirt/_/Java) (a hit for [Al Hirt](http://www.last.fm/music/Al+Hirt)) and [Whipped Cream](http: //www.last.fm/music/Herb+Alpert+and+the+Tijuana+Brass/_/Whipped+Cream) which went platinum when done by [Herb Alpert](http://www.last.fm/music/Herb+Alpert). Other Toussaint compositions that continue to be covered by artists to this day, include “[Working In A Coalmine](http://www.last.fm/music/Lee+Dorsey/_/Working+In+A+Coalmine)”, “[Play Something Sweet (Brickyard Blues)](http://www.last.fm/music/Three+Dog+N ight/_/Play+Something+Sweet+\(Brickyard+Blues\))”, “[Get Out My Life Woman](ht tp://www.last.fm/music/The+Paul+Butterfield+Blues+Band/_/Get+Out+My+Life+Woman )” and “[Yes We Can Can](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Pointer+Sisters/_/Yes+We+Can+Can)”. Some of his songs from the 1950s and 1960s were penned under his mother’s maiden name, the pseudonym Naomi Neville. These include [Fortune Teller](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Rolling+Stones/_/Fortune+Teller) recorded by [The Rolling Stones](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Rolling+Stones), and “[Ruler of My Heart](http://www.last.fm/music/Irma+Thomas/_/Ruler+of+My+Heart)”, recorded by [Irma Thomas](http://www.last.fm/music/Irma+Thomas) and reinterpreted as “[Pain In My Heart](http://www.last.fm/music/Otis+Redding/_/Pain+In+My+Heart)” by Otis Redding. He arranged horn parts for [The Band](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Band), and wrote what became one of [Glen Campbell](http://www.last.fm/music/Glen+Campbell)’s biggest hits [Southern Nights](http://www.last.fm/music/Glen+Campbell/_/Southern+Nights) as well as [A Certain Girl](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Yardbirds/_/A+Certain+Girl) , which charted when recorded by acts as diverse as [Warren Zevon](http://www.last.fm/music/Warren+Zevon), [Benny Spellman](http://www.last.fm/music/Benny+Spellman) and [The Yardbirds](http://www.last
