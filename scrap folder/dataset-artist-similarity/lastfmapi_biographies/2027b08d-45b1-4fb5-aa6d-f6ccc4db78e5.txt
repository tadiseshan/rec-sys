Horace Andy is a legendary [roots reggae](http://www.last.fm/tag/roots%20reggae) singer, notable for such tracks as _[Government Land](http://www.last.fm/music/Horace+Andy/_/Government+Land)_, _[You Are My Angel](http://www.last.fm/music/Horace+Andy/_/You+Are+My+Angel)_ and _[Skylarking](http://www.last.fm/music/Horace+Andy/_/Skylarking)_.      Born Horace Hinds on 19 February 1951 in Kingston, [Jamaica](http://www.last.fm/place/Jamaica), he made his earliest recordings in the late 1960s. Known for his distinctive [falsetto](http://www.last.fm/tag/falsetto) vocal style, he sung on many classic production for reggae producers, including [Phil Pratt](http://www.last.fm/music/Phil+Pratt), [King Tubby](http://www.last.fm/music/King+Tubby) and [Prince Jammy](http://www.last.fm/music/Prince+Jammy). He is a devout Rastafari and, like many reggae singers, much of his material deals with themes of religion and social justice.      Andy found a new generation of fans in the 1990s, thanks to his work with [trip hop](http://www.last.fm/tag/trip%20hop) pioneers [Massive Attack](http://www.last.fm/music/Massive+Attack).
