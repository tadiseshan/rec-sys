**Freedy Johnston** (born 1961) is a New York City-based [singer-songwriter](http://www.last.fm/tag/singer-songwriter) originally from Kinsley, Kansas. Johnston’s first album, “[The Trouble Tree](http://www.last.fm/music/Freedy+Johnston/The+Trouble+Tree)” was released in 1990, and features the guitar playing of the roots musician and author, [David Hamburger](http://www.last.fm/music/David+Hamburger). Johnston sold some of his family’s farmland to finance the recording of his second album, [Can You Fly](http://www.last.fm/music/Freedy+Johnston/Can+You+Fly) (an event he wrote about in a song on that album, “[Trying to Tell You I Don’t Know](http://www.last.fm/music/Freedy+Johnston/_/Trying+to+Tell+You+I+Don%27t+Know)”). Johnston followed up with his 1994 major label debut [This Perfect World](http://www.last.fm/music/Freedy+Johnston/This+Perfect+World), which received rave reviews and led to Rolling Stone Magazine naming him “songwriter of the year”. That album also spawned a minor alternative hit with the song “[Bad Reputation](http://www.last.fm/music/Freedy+Johnston/_/Bad+Reputation)”.      Johnston’s songs are often about [troubled](http://www.last.fm/tag/troubled) [loners](http://www.last.fm/tag/loners), and cover topics like [heartbreak](http://www.last.fm/tag/heartbreak), [alienation](http://www.last.fm/tag/alienation) and [disappointment](http://www.last.fm/tag/disappointment). Known for the craftsmanship of his songs, he has sometimes been called a “songwriter’s songwriter”.      He has contributed songs to the soundtracks for movies including _Kingpin_ and _Things to Do in Denver When You’re Dead_. Johnston and fellow musicians Jay Moran, James “Pie” Cowan, Duke Erikson, and [Butch Vig](http://www.last.fm/music/Butch+Vig) perform occasional shows as a [covers](http://www.last.fm/tag/covers) band called “The Know-It-All Boyfriends.”      Johnston latest release is a covers album titled “[Rain On the City](http://www.last.fm/music/Freedy+Johnston/Rain+On+the+City)”, It includes selections from Tom Petty, Paul McCartney, Matthew Sweet and The Hollies.
