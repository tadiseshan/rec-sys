**Apache Indian** is the stage name of [dancehall](http://www.last.fm/tag/dancehall)/[reggae](http://www.last.fm/tag/reggae)/[pop](http://www.last.fm/tag/pop) DJ and singer Steven Kapur (born on 11 May 1967 in Handsworth, Birmingham, best known for his iconic international hit “[Boomshackalak](http://www.last.fm/music/Apache+Indian/_/Boomshackalak)” (also known by many other titles such as “[Boom Shack-A-Lak](http://www.last.fm/music/Apache+Indian/_/Boom+Shack-A-Lak)”).      He introduced a combination of the traditional Indian [bhangra](http://www.last.fm/tag/bhangra) music with the style of [raggamuffin](http://www.last.fm/tag/raggamuffin), the mix-up often known as [bhangramuffin](http://www.last.fm/tag/bhangramuffin), to the world with his first album, 1993’s ‘_[No Reservations](http://www.last.fm/music/Apache+Indian/No+Reservations)_’. That release was produced by Simon & Diamond, Phil Chill, Robert Livingston, Bobby Digital, and Sly Dunbar. It was followed by ‘_[Make Way For The Indian](http://www.last.fm/music/Apache+Indian/Make+Way+For+The+Indian)_’, produced by Sly & Robbie, The Press, Mafia & Fluxy, Pandit Dinesh, and Chris Lane. That album notably featured rapper [Tim Dog](http://www.last.fm/music/Tim+Dog) and spawned the massive hit [Boom Shack-A-Lak](http://www.last.fm/music/Apache+Indian/_/Boom+Shack-A-Lak)”, which has been used in several movies and has become a sort of ‘[signature song](http://www.last.fm/tag/signature%20song)’ for Apache Indian .      ‘_[Real People](http://www.last.fm/music/Apache+Indian/Real+People)_’, produced by Harjinder Noparat, proved to be his most experimental album and also featured more Indian elements than many of his previous releases. ‘_[Karma](http://www.last.fm/music/Apache+Indian/Karma)_’, his first album of the new century, was a return to more traditional forms of [reggae](http://www.last.fm/tag/reggae). That album featured Luciano, Boy George, and Mafia & Fluxy.      As stated before, Apache Indian merged raggamuffin with Indian music as well as influences from mainstream [dance](http://www.last.fm/tag/dance) music and [pop rap](http://www.last.fm/tag/pop%20rap). Born in Handsworth, Birmingham, England. He helped to start a trend that has found a huge following. He’s also the only Asian to date to have seven Top 40 Hits on the mainstream British charts. Stylistically, Apache Indian generally moved from mixing Indian music with raggamuffin and commercial rap to exploring more traditional forms of reggae on his most recent albums.      Lyrically, Apache Indian - who always uses Jamaican English - is known for his sharp social criticism (“Arranged Marriage” featuring Natacha Atlas; “Mention”) as well as his party tracks (“Boomshackalak”, “Jump Up”).      Apache Indian has recorded with Boyz II Men, Blackstreet, Wreckx ‘n’ Effect, UB40, US chart topper Sean Paul, Maxi Priest, General Levy, Brian and Tony Gold, Shaggy, Yami Bolo, Boy George, A.R.
