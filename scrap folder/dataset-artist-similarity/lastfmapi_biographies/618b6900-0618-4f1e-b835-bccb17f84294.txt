**Eric Clapton** (born Eric Patrick Clapton in Ripley, Surrey, England on 30 March 1945), nicknamed “Slowhand”, is a Grammy Award winning English composer, singer and [guitarist](http://www.last.fm/tag/guitarist) who became one of the most respected artists of the rock era, winning three inductions into the [rock and roll hall of fame](http://www.last.fm/tag/rock%20and%20roll%20hall%20of%20fame).       Clapton’s musical style has changed during his career, but has remained rooted in the [blues](http://www.last.fm/tag/blues). [Clapton](http://www.last.fm/music/+noredirect/Clapton) is credited as an innovator in several phases of his career, which have included [blues- rock](http://www.last.fm/tag/blues-rock) with [John Mayall & The Bluesbreakers](http://www.last.fm/music/John+Mayall+&+The+Bluesbreakers) and [The Yardbirds](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Yardbirds) (1963-1965), early single hit “Come With Me” by The Roosters, blues-hard rock (with [Cream](http://www.last.fm/music/Cream)), and work as a sideman and a solo artist; he has achieved chart success in genres ranging from [delta blues](http://www.last.fm/tag/delta%20blues) (_Unplugged_) and [psychedelic rock](http://www.last.fm/tag/psychedelic%20rock) (_Sunshine of Your Love_) to [pop](http://www.last.fm/tag/pop) (_Change the World_) and [reggae](http://www.last.fm/tag/reggae) (_I Shot the Sheriff_).      [Eric Clapton](http://www.last.fm/music/Eric+Clapton) was a friend of [George Harrison](http://www.last.fm/music/George+Harrison) of the **Beatles**. It is believed that he wrote [Layla](http://www.last.fm/music/Eric+Clapton/_/Layla) about Harrison’s wife, Pattie Boyd, whom he married in 1979 after she divorced George Harrison. They divorced in 1988. Eric Clapton has also dated singers Marianne Faithfull and Rosanne Cash, actresses Patsy Kensit and Sharon Stone, and rock muses Cynrinda Fox and Geraldine Edwards, who was one of the inspirations for Penny Lane in Almost Famous. He is now married to Melia McEnry and has been since 2002. They have three daughters together.      Clapton formed relationships with different artists in his career. From his start in [The Yardbirds](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Yardbirds), then journeying forward to [Cream](http://www.last.fm/music/Cream), [John Mayall](http://www.last.fm/music/John+Mayall) and the Bluesbreakers, [Derrek and the Dominoes](http://www.last.fm/music/Derrek+and+the+Dominoes), he is an extremely gifted musician. Clapton would go on to play lead guitar in [Roger Waters](http://www.last.fm/music/Roger+Waters)’ first solo album, The Pros and Cons of Hitchiking. All this while compiling hit after hit in his solo career, has made Clapton one of the most enduring guitarists of the classic rock era.      “[Tears in Heaven](http://www.last.fm/music/Eric+Clapton/_/Tears+in+Heaven)” was written after his son’s tragic death. It was co-written with [Russ Titelman](http://www.last.fm/music/Russ+Titelman) and acknowledged with a Grammy in 1993
