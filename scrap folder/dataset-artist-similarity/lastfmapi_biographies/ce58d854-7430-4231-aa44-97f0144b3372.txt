Sarah McLachlan (born Sarah Ann McLachlan January 28, 1968 in Halifax, Nova Scotia, Canada) is a Grammy-winning musician, singer and songwriter.      She is known for the emotional sound of her ballads. Some of her most popular songs include “Angel”, “Building a Mystery”, “Adia”, “Possession”, “Fallen”, “I Will Remember You”, and “World on Fire”. Her best-selling album to date is [Surfacing](http://www.last.fm/music/Sarah+McLachlan/Surfacing), for which she won two Grammy Awards and four Juno Awards.      Her first album [Touch](http://www.last.fm/music/Sarah+McLachlan/Touch) was released in September 28, 1988, recorded in 1987-1988, Vancouver, Canada. The album received both critical and commercial success and included the hit song [Vox](http://www.last.fm/music/Sarah+McLachlan/_/Vox). During this period she co-operated with Tom Ferris and Dean Russel of [Moev](http://www.last.fm/music/Moev), who discovered her as a singer. She contributed backing vocals their 1990 album [Head Down](http://www.last.fm/music/Moev/Head+Down) and embarked on her first national concert tour as an opening act for [The Grapes Of Wrath](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Grapes+Of+Wrath), another Tom Ferris’s band.      Her second album [Solace](http://www.last.fm/music/Sarah+McLachlan/Solace) (1991) was a real breakthrough in Canada, spawning the hit singles [The Path of Thorns (Terms)](http://www.last.fm/music/Sarah+McLachlan/_/The+Path+of+Thor ns+\(Terms\)) and [Into the Fire](http://www.last.fm/music/Sarah+McLachlan/_/Into+the+Fire) and being certified double platinum for sales of 200,000 copies in Canada.      In 1994 her third album [Fumbling Towards Ecstasy](http://www.last.fm/music/Sarah+McLachlan/Fumbling+Towards+Ecstasy) quietly became Sarah’s international breakthrough, scaling the charts in a number of countries. In the United States, the album was a steady seller that stayed in the middle ranges of the pop charts for almost two years.      Sarah’s fourth album [Surfacing](http://www.last.fm/music/Sarah+McLachlan/Surfacing), released in 1997, brought her international success, selling over 11 million copies worldwide. Still in the spotlight from the album, Sarah launched the highly popular [Lilith Fair](http://www.sarahmclachlan.com/lilith-fair-tickets/) tour, which helped launch the careers of several well-known female artists.      Her next record [Afterglow](http://www.last.fm/music/Sarah+McLachlan/Afterglow) came out in 2003; it contained the singles [Fallen](http://www.last.fm/music/Sarah+McLachlan/_/Fallen), [Stupid](http://www.last.fm/music/Sarah+McLachlan/_/Stupid), and [World on Fire](http://www.last.fm/music/Sarah+McLachlan/_/World+on+Fire). It was her first album of new material in six years. Sarah took a few years furlough to start a family with her husband and drummer, Ashwin Sood.      Her Holiday album [Wintersong](http://www.last.fm/music/Sarah+McLachlan/Wintersong) came out in October 2006
