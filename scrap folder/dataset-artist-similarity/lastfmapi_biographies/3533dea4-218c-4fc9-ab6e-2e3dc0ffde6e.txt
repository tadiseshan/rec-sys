Pete Yorn (b. July 27, 1974) is an American [singer- songwriter](http://www.last.fm/tag/singer-songwriter) and guitarist originally from Montville, New Jersey, United States.      Yorn started as a drummer who also played guitar. In 1990, he was drumming for his band in a high school talent contest when someone suggested he sing the Replacements song “[Talent Show](http://www.last.fm/music/+noredirect/Replacements/_/Talent+Show).” That went over so well, a competing band asked him to sing Neil Young’s “[Rockin’ in the Free World](http://www.last.fm/music/Neil+Young/_/Rockin%27+in+the+Free+World)” with them.      He was first recognized when the Farrelly brothers picked “[Strange Condition](http://www.last.fm/music/Pete+Yorn/_/Strange+Condition)” for their film Me, Myself & Irene.      He has gained increasing popularity following the use of his song “[Just Another](http://www.last.fm/music/Pete+Yorn/_/Just+Another)” on popular WB television show _Dawson’s Creek_.      Another of his songs, [Murray](http://www.last.fm/music/Pete+Yorn/_/Murray) was used in the credits of the film _Igby Goes Down_      The song “[Undercover](http://www.last.fm/music/Pete+Yorn/_/Undercover)” was used in the first Spiderman movie and appears on the soundtrack.      The song “[Lose You](http://www.last.fm/music/Pete+Yorn/_/Lose+You)” was used in the closing of an episode of _House_ on Fox.
