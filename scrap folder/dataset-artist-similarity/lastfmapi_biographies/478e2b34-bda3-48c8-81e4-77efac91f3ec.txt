Tanya Donelly (born July 14, 1966, in Newport, Rhode Island) is an American singer/songwriter and guitarist based in New England who cofounded [Throwing Muses](http://www.last.fm/music/Throwing+Muses) with stepsister [Kristin Hersh](http://www.last.fm/music/Kristin+Hersh), then went on to work in [The Breeders](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Breeders) and [Belly](http://www.last.fm/music/Belly) in the 1990s. In the late 1990s, she settled into a solo recording career, working largely with musicians connected to the Boston music scene.      Donelly is best known for her Grammy-nominated work in the mid-1990s as lead vocalist and songwriter for [Belly](http://www.last.fm/music/Belly), when she scored a national radio and music television hit with her composition “Feed the Tree.” [Belly](http://www.last.fm/music/Belly) recorded on Sire/Reprise Records and 4AD; Donelly’s solo works have been released on Warner Brothers Records and 4AD.      Over the years she has listed several musical influences. In one interview she named her guitar-playing influences as [Marc Ribot](http://www.last.fm/music/Marc+Ribot), [The Beatles](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Beatles), and former bandmate Hersh. More recently she mentioned [Leonard Cohen](http://www.last.fm/music/Leonard+Cohen) as a songwriting hero, citing her then-current listening favorites as [Lucinda Williams](http://www.last.fm/music/Lucinda+Williams) and [Joan Wasser](http://www.last.fm/music/Joan+Wasser), and listing Boston-based groups like [The Dambuilders](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Dambuilders), [Pixies](http://www.last.fm/music/Pixies), and [Count Zero](http://www.last.fm/music/Count+Zero) as past favorites. Although Donelly mainly performs her own original songs, she has in recent years added covers by [Robyn Hitchcock](http://www.last.fm/music/Robyn+Hitchcock), [Nina Simone](http://www.last.fm/music/Nina+Simone), and [The Beatles](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Beatles) to her repertoire.
