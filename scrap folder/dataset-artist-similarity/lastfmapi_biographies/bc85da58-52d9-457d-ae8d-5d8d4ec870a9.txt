Aaliyah Dana Haughton (January 16, 1979 - August 25, 2001) was an American R&B / Pop singer, dancer, fashion model and actress. Born in Brooklyn, New York, she was raised in Detroit, Michigan, where she attended Detroit School of Performing Arts. The 22 year old singer’s life was tragically cut short in a 2001 plane crash after filming her video for “[Rock the Boat](http://www.last.fm/music/Aaliyah/_/Rock+the+Boat)” in The Bahamas.      Introduced to audiences by R&B singer [R. Kelly](http://www.last.fm/music/R.+Kelly), Aaliyah became famous in her own right during the mid-1990s with several hit records from the songwriting/production team of [Missy Elliott](http://www.last.fm/music/Missy+Elliott) and [Timbaland](http://www.last.fm/music/Timbaland), and their associate Steve “Static” Garrett. Notable for recording several hit records, Aaliyah has sold over 32 million records worldwide. The [Enough Said Songfacts](http://www.songfacts.com/detail.php?id=26909) reports that a second posthumous album will be released by the end of 2012. The record will be ci- executive produced by [Drake](http://www.last.fm/music/Drake) and Noah “40” Shebib and Drake premiered the first single, “[Enough Said](http://www.last.fm/music/Aaliyah/_/Enough+Said),” at the OVO Fest in Toronto, on August 5, 2012.
