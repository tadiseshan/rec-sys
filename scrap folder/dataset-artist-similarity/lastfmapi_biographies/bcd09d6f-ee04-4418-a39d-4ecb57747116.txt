Lord Finesse is a [Hip Hop](http://www.last.fm/tag/hip+hop) artist and producer, hailing from The Bronx, New York. In 1989, Finesse, and his former partner [DJ Mike Smooth](http://www.last.fm/music/DJ+Mike+Smooth), signed to Wild Pitch Records, home to other popular Hip Hop artists like [Gang Starr](http://www.last.fm/music/Gang+Starr), [Main Source](http://www.last.fm/music/Main+Source), [Chill Rob G](http://www.last.fm/music/Chill+Rob+G), [Percee P](http://www.last.fm/music/Percee+P), and [O.C.](http://www.last.fm/music/O.C.). In 1990, the duo released their debut album Funky Technician. He was hailed for his strong lyrical ability and clever punchline delivery. The album featured production from future star beat-makers [DJ Premier](http://www.last.fm/music/DJ+Premier), [Diamond D](http://www.last.fm/music/Diamond+D) and [Showbiz](http://www.last.fm/music/Showbiz). Finesse became the leader of the popular New York underground crew [D.I.T.C.](http://www.last.fm/music/D.I.T.C.), an acronym for ‘Diggin In The Crates’. Future members included [Showbiz & A.G.](http://www.last.fm/music/Showbiz+&+A.G.), [Diamond D](http://www.last.fm/music/Diamond+D), [Fat Joe](http://www.last.fm/music/Fat+Joe), O.C., [Buckwild](http://www.last.fm/music/Buckwild) and the late [Big L](http://www.last.fm/music/Big+L). The crew is responsible for releasing some of the most heralded [underground hip-hop](http://www.last.fm/tag/underground +hip-hop) albums of the 90’s, including Show & AG’s Runaway Slave, O.C.’s Word…Life and Big L’s Lifestylez Ov Da Poor & Dangerous.      Finesse returned as a solo artist in late 1991 with his sophomore effort, Return Of The Funky Man. The album featured guest appearances from Percee P and AG. The albums title track peaked at #13 on the Hot Rap Singles chart. In 1992, Finesse discovered a young rapper freestyling in the back of a local record store, the young kid went by the name of Big L. He enlisted the young rapper to make an appearance on the remix to his song “Yes You May”. In 1994, Finesse make a production appearance on The Notorious B.I.G.’s classic debut Ready to Die, on the track “Suicidal Thoughts”. In 1995, he produced a portion of Big L’s debut album Lifestylez Ov Da Poor & Dangerous, including the hit single “M.V.P.”. He finally returned as an artist in 1996 with the acclaimed album The Awakening. Finesse produced the entire album himself, and enlisted a large number of guests, including O.C., [KRS-One](http://www.last.fm/music /KRS-One), [MC Lyte](http://www.last.fm/music/MC+Lyte), [Akinyele](http://www.last.fm/music/Akinyele), Showbiz and A.G., Diamond D, and [Kid Capri](http://www.last.fm/music/Kid+Capri).
