France Gall (born Isabelle Geneviève Marie Anne Gall on 9 October, 1947 in Paris, France) is an influential singer that has performed for many decades. She notably represented Luxembourg in the 1965 Eurovision Song Contest with “[Poupée de cire, poupée de son](http://www.last.fm/music/France+Gall/_/Poup%C 3%A9e+de+cire,+poup%C3%A9e+de+son)”; that winning song is just one of many that she has performed written by [Serge Gainsbourg](http://www.last.fm/music/Serge+Gainsbourg). Her career spans roughly forty years, primarily in France, but she is best known over the world for the songs she that performed in the [60s](http://www.last.fm/tag/60s), many of them a part of the [ye-ye](http://www.last.fm/tag/ye-ye) style. She sings in both French and English.      Besides the highly successful “[Poupée de cire, poupée de son](http://www.last .fm/music/France+Gall/_/Poup%C3%A9e+de+cire,+poup%C3%A9e+de+son)”, she’s also notably sung “[Les Sucettes](http://www.last.fm/music/France+Gall/_/Les+Sucettes)” and “[Baby Pop](http://www.last.fm/music/France+Gall/_/Baby+Pop)”. In France, she is perhaps more known for the [chanson](http://www.last.fm/tag/chanson) songs she sang in the late-[70s](http://www.last.fm/tag/70s) through the mid-[80s](http://www.last.fm/tag/80s), many of them which written by her husband, [Michel Berger](http://www.last.fm/music/Michel+Berger), who is now deceased. In 1987, she had some additional international success with her [Ella Fitzgerald](http://www.last.fm/music/Ella+Fitzgerald) tribute “[Ella, elle l’a](http://www.last.fm/music/France+Gall/_/Ella,+elle+l%27a)”. She still has recorded music into the new millennium.
