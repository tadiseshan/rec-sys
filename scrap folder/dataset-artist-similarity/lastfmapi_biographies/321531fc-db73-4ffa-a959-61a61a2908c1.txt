Erykah Badu (born Erica Abi Wright on February 26, 1971 in Dallas, Texas) is an American R’n’B/[neo-soul](http://www.last.fm/tag/neo-soul) artist whose work crosses over into [jazz](http://www.last.fm/tag/jazz).      She is best known for her singles [You Got Me](http://www.last.fm/music/Erykah+Badu/_/You+Got+Me) her collaboration with [The Roots](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Roots), as well as her own songs [Tyrone](http://www.last.fm/music/Erykah+Badu/_/Tyrone), [Love Of My Life](http://www.last.fm/music/Erykah+Badu/_/Love+Of+My+Life), [On & On](http://www.last.fm/music/Erykah+Badu/_/On+&+On), [Bag Lady](http://www.last.fm/music/Erykah+Badu/_/Bag+Lady), [The Healer](http://www.last.fm/music/Erykah+Badu/_/The+Healer), [Honey](http://www.last.fm/music/Erykah+Badu/_/Honey) and [Soldier](http://www.last.fm/music/Erykah+Badu/_/Soldier). Her lyrics are highly personal urban philosophy which throw emotional challenges in the face of the listener. She weaves unusual musical influences together creating a rich texture of sound.      Some music journalists have labeled her [nu soul](http://www.last.fm/tag/nu%20soul), [nouvelle- soul](http://www.last.fm/tag/nouvelle-soul) or [neo soul](http://www.last.fm/tag/neo%20soul), often comparing her to [Billie Holiday](http://www.last.fm/music/Billie+Holiday) in lyrical delivery and grouping her with [Maxwell](http://www.last.fm/music/Maxwell) and [D’Angelo](http://www.last.fm/music/D%27Angelo) in musical genre.      [Baduizm](http://www.last.fm/music/Erykah+Badu/Baduizm), Badu’s highly acclaimed debut album, was released in early 1997 and debuted at #2 on the Billboard charts. Lead single [On & On](http://www.last.fm/music/Erykah+Badu/_/On+&+On) reached #12 on the singles charts in both the U.S. and UK. Badu received notice for her introspective lyrics and jazzy, bass-heavy sound, and was hailed as one of the leading lights of the burgeoning neo soul genre. Her sophisticated style of singing drew many comparisons to [Billie Holiday](http://www.last.fm/music/Billie+Holiday). Baduizm eventually went triple platinum and, along with On & On, won Grammy Awards at the 1998 ceremonies.      During that year, Badu became involved with rapper [André 3000](http://www.last.fm/music/+noredirect/Andr%C3%A9+3000) of [OutKast](http://www.last.fm/music/OutKast), with whom she had a child, Seven, who was born in 1997. Their relationship ended sometime in the late 90s. Badu recorded her first live album, [Live](http://www.last.fm/music/Erykah+Badu/Live), while pregnant with Seven, and the release of the recording coincided with the birth to her child. Live reached #4 on the Billboard charts, selling double platinum, and spawned another R&B hit single in [Tyrone](http://www.last.fm/music/Erykah+Badu/_/Tyrone) a song chiding a selfish, cheap, and inattentive boyfriend.
