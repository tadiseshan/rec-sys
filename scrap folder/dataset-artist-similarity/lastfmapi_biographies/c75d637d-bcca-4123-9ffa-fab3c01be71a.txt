**Vanessa-Mae Vanakorn Nicholson** (born October 27, 1978 in [Singapore](http://www.last.fm/place/Singapore), currently lives in [United Kingdom](http://www.last.fm/place/United+Kingdom)), known on stage as **Vanessa-Mae** (in Chinese: 陳美, Chén Měi) is an internationally known British [pop](http://www.last.fm/tag/pop) and [classical](http://www.last.fm/tag/classical) musician, especially noted for her violin skills. Her music style is self-described as “violin techno-acoustic fusion” as several of her albums prominently feature the [techno](http://www.last.fm/tag/techno) style.      Vanessa-Mae was born in [Singapore](http://www.last.fm/place/Singapore) to a Thai father (Varaprong Vanakorn) and a Chinese mother (Pamela Tan). After her parents separated, her mother married Englishman Graham Nicholson, and the family moved to England when Vanessa-Mae was four years old. She grew up in London and is a British citizen.      Vanessa-Mae began playing piano at the age of three and violin at five. She was relatively famous in the [United Kingdom](http://www.last.fm/place/United+Kingdom) throughout her childhood making regular appearances on television (for example on “Blue Peter”) mostly involving [classical](http://www.last.fm/tag/classical) music and conservative style. According to Guinness World Records, she is the youngest soloist to record both the [Ludwig van Beethoven](http://www.last.fm/music/Ludwig+van+Beethoven) and [Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky](http://www.last.fm/music/Pyotr+Ilyich+Tchaikovsky) violin concertos, a feat she accomplished at the age of thirteen. During this time she attended the Francis Holland School in central London.      Vanessa-Mae made her international professional debut at the Schleswig- Holstein Musik Festival in [Germany](http://www.last.fm/place/Germany) in 1988, and also during 1988 made her concerto debut on stage with the Philharmonia Orchestra in London.      On entering adolescence Vanessa-Mae broke away from her traditional [classical](http://www.last.fm/tag/classical) influences and became known for her flashy, sexy style appearing in music videos in stylish outfits. She appeared on the [Janet Jackson](http://www.last.fm/music/Janet+Jackson) album [The Velvet Rope](http://www.last.fm/music/Janet+Jackson/The+Velvet+Rope) playing a violin solo on the song “[Velvet Rope](http://www.last.fm/music/Janet+Jackson/_/Velvet+Rope)”. Her first pop- style album, [The Violin Player](http://www.last.fm/music/Vanessa- Mae/The+Violin+Player), was released in 1995, and is still regarded as one of the best works of her career.      In April 2006, Vanessa-Mae was ranked as the wealthiest young entertainer under 30 in the UK in the Sunday Times Rich List 2006, having an estimated fortune of about £32 million ($64 million) stemming from concerts and record sales of over an estimated 10 million copies worldwide, which is an unprecedented achievement for a young female violinist
