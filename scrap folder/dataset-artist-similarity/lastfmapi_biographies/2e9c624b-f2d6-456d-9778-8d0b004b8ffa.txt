Ayub Ogada is a musician from [Kenya](http://www.last.fm/place/Kenya). A singer, he also performs on the traditional east African stringed instrument, the [nyatiti](http://www.last.fm/tag/nyatiti). Of the Luo people, he was born in Mombasa.      Ogada arrived in the United Kingdom in 1986, and in 1993 recorded the album [En Mana Kuoyo](http://www.last.fm/music/Ayub+Ogada/En+Mana+Kuoyo) on [Peter Gabriel](http://www.last.fm/music/Peter+Gabriel)’s [Real World](http://www.last.fm/label/Real+World) label. His music has also been heard on the soundtrack of the 2005 film The Constant Gardener and collaborates with [Susheela Raman](http://www.last.fm/music/Susheela+Raman) on several tracks on her “[Salt Rain](http://www.last.fm/music/Susheela+Raman/_/Salt+Rain)” album, such as “[O Rama](http://www.last.fm/music/Susheela+Raman/_/O+Rama)” . In addition, Ayub Ogada has collaborated with the [Afro Celt Sound System](http://www.last.fm/music/Afro+Celt+Sound+System) on their first and fourth releases.
