Isobel Campbell (born April 27, 1976) is a Scottish singer, [cellist](http://www.last.fm/tag/cellist) and [composer](http://www.last.fm/tag/composer) in the [indie](http://www.last.fm/tag/indie) and [folk](http://www.last.fm/tag/folk) genres. As with her former band [Belle and Sebastian](http://www.last.fm/music/Belle+and+Sebastian), she is often labelled as [twee pop](http://www.last.fm/tag/twee%20pop). Campbell was a member of [Belle and Sebastian](http://www.last.fm/music/Belle+and+Sebastian) from their formation in Glasgow in 1996 until 2002, when she departed the band for personal reasons. Isobel played [cello](http://www.last.fm/tag/cello) with Belle and Sebastian and sang backing vocals. She also took lead vocals on a few songs from the band, and co-wrote their top-20 U.K. single Legal Man.      In 2006 Isobel released a collaborative LP ([Ballad of the Broken Seas](http:/ /www.last.fm/music/Isobel+Campbell+&+Mark+Lanegan/Ballad+of+the+Broken+Seas)) with former [Screaming Trees](http://www.last.fm/music/Screaming+Trees) and [Queens of the Stone Age](http://www.last.fm/music/Queens+of+the+Stone+Age) singer [Mark Lanegan](http://www.last.fm/music/Mark+Lanegan), a mixture of folk and more rock-orientated music. October 2006 also saw the release of her latest album, Milkwhite Sheets, a more acoustic folk album, consisting of a mixture of traditional songs alonside Isobel’s own compositions.      Isobel’s band [The Gentle Waves](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Gentle+Waves) released its first album on [jeepster](http://www.last.fm/tag/jeepster) Records, titled The Green Fields of Foreverland, in 1999. The second Gentle Waves record, Swansong For You came out one year later. In 2002, she collaborated with Scottish [jazz](http://www.last.fm/tag/jazz) musician [Bill Wells](http://www.last.fm/music/Bill+Wells) on a collection of [Billie Holliday](http://www.last.fm/music/+noredirect/Billie+Holliday) songs, released by Creeping Bent.      In 2003 Campbell released [Amorino](http://www.last.fm/music/Isobel+Campbell/Amorino), the first solo album under her own name, to very good reviews. Bill Wells was featured here again, along with other jazz musicians.      [Isobel Campbell & Mark Lanegan](http://www.last.fm/music/Isobel+Campbell+&+Mark+Lanegan) form a unique combination of gruff vocals and dulcet voices. They have released three albums so far: [Ballad of the Broken Seas](http://www.last.fm/music/Isobel+Cam pbell+&+Mark+Lanegan/Ballad+of+the+Broken+Seas), which received a nomination in the 2006 Mercury Awards, [Sunday At Devil Dirt](http://www.last.fm/music/Is obel+Campbell+&+Mark+Lanegan/Sunday+At+Devil+Dirt), which was released in May 2008. The album received US release in Fall 2008. And finally their third album [Hawk](http://www.last.fm/music/Isobel+Campbell+&+Mark+Lanegan/Hawk) was released in August 2010.
