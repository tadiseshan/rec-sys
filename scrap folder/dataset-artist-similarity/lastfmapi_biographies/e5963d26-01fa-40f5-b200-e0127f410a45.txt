**Harry Edward Nilsson III** (born June 15, 1941 in Brooklyn, New York - died January 15, 1994), sometimes credited simply as **Nilsson**, was an American [singer-songwriter](http://www.last.fm/tag/singer-songwriter) with a three octave vocal range. He won Grammy Awards for Best Contemporary Male Vocal Performance for “[Everybody’s Talkin’](http://www.last.fm/music/Harry+Nilsson/_/Everybody%27s+Talkin%27)” (1970) and “[Without You](http://www.last.fm/music/Harry+Nilsson/_/Without+You)” (1973), also his two biggest hits. Although both the aforementioned songs were composed by others, he was a talented songwriter in his own right, having written “[One](http://www.last.fm/music/Harry+Nilsson/_/One)”, a hit single for [Three Dog Night](http://www.last.fm/music/Three+Dog+Night). His early offerings are oft compared to [The Beatles](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Beatles)’ [psychedelic](http://www.last.fm/tag/psychedelic) period; indeed, The Beatles were themselves outspoken fans of Nilsson, helping him early on in his career.      Nilsson’s work has seen much use in film; notably, “Everybody’s Talking” (originally written by [Fred Neil](http://www.last.fm/music/Fred+Neil)) featured prominently in _[Midnight Cowboy](http://www.last.fm/music/Various+Artists/Midnight+Cowboy)_. “Without You” (originally written by [Pete Ham](http://www.last.fm/music/Pete+Ham) and [Tom Evans](http://www.last.fm/music/Tom+Evans) of [Badfinger](http://www.last.fm/music/Badfinger)) also appeared in _[The Rules of Attraction](http://www.last.fm/music/Various+Artists/The+Rules+of+Attractio n)_. Nilsson wrote and performed the music for the 1971 animated television film _[The Point!](http://www.last.fm/music/Harry+Nilsson/The+Point!)_; some versions of the film features narration by [Ringo Starr](http://www.last.fm/music/Ringo+Starr), one of Nilsson’s closest friends. Other films that have utilized Nilsson songs include _[Goodfellas](http://www.last.fm/music/Various+Artists/Goodfellas)_, _[Reservoir Dogs](http://www.last.fm/music/Various+Artists/Reservoir+Dogs)_, and _[You’ve Got Mail](http://www.last.fm/music/Various+Artists/You%27ve+Got+Mail)_.      Nilsson died unexpectedly of heart failure in 1994, after a night of recording for what was intended to be his first studio album in over a decade, provisionally titled _Papa’s Got a Brown New Robe_. Nilsson remains a beloved artist amongst songwriters themselves, being the subject of a tribute cover album featuring such luminaries as [Brian Wilson](http://www.last.fm/music/Brian+Wilson), [Randy Newman](http://www.last.fm/music/Randy+Newman), and Ringo Starr. 2010 saw the domestic release of the biographical documentary, _Who Is Harry Nilsson? (And Why Is Everybody Talkin’ About Him?)_, featuring interviews with friends, family, and colleagues, such as [Micky Dolenz](http://www.last.fm/music/Micky+Dolenz), [Eric Idle](http://www.last.fm/music/Eric+Idle), and [Paul Williams](http://www.last.fm/music/Paul+Williams).
