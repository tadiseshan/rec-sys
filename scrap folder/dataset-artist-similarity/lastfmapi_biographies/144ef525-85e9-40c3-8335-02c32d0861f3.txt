There are two artists with the name John Mayer; American pop singer-songwriter John Clayton Mayer (b. 1977), and Anglo-Indian Composer John Jiddhu Mayer (1930 - 2004) and leader of [John Mayer’s Indo Jazz Fusions](http://www.last.fm/music/John+Mayer%27s+Indo+Jazz+Fusions)      1) **John Clayton Mayer** was born October 16, 1977, in Fairfield, Connecticut, USA, and started playing guitar at 13 after being inspired by a [Stevie Ray Vaughan](http://www.last.fm/music/Stevie+Ray+Vaughan) tape his neighbor gave him. In 1998 he moved to Atlanta, Georgia where he refined his skills and gained a following.      In 2003, he won the Grammy Award for Best Male Pop Vocal Performance for the 2002 single “[Your Body Is a Wonderland](http://www.last.fm/music/John+Mayer/_/Your+Body+Is+a+Wonderland)” from the album _[Room for Squares](http://www.last.fm/music/John+Mayer/Room+for+Squares)_.      In February 2005, he was awarded the Song of the Year Grammy for his song [Daughters](http://www.last.fm/music/John+Mayer/_/Daughters), which he composed while in the shower, from the album _[Heavier Things](http://www.last.fm/music/John+Mayer/Heavier+Things)_. In winning the award, he beat out such contenders as [Alicia Keys](http://www.last.fm/music/Alicia+Keys), and [Kanye West](http://www.last.fm/music/Kanye+West). He dedicated this award to his grandmother, Annie Hoffman, who died in May 2004. He also won Best Male Pop Vocal Performance, for which [Elvis Costello](http://www.last.fm/music/Elvis+Costello), [Prince](http://www.last.fm/music/Prince) and [Seal](http://www.last.fm/music/Seal) were also nominated. In 2007, John won 2 Grammys, one for Best Male Pop Vocal Performance, for the song “Waiting On The World To Change,” and also Best Pop Vocal Album for his album Continuum.      To date, Mayer has toured with many groups, including [Maroon 5](http://www.last.fm/music/Maroon+5), [Guster](http://www.last.fm/music/Guster), [Counting Crows](http://www.last.fm/music/Counting+Crows), [The Wallflowers](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Wallflowers), [Teitur](http://www.last.fm/music/Teitur), [Ben Folds](http://www.last.fm/music/Ben+Folds), and [Sheryl Crow](http://www.last.fm/music/Sheryl+Crow).      In 2004, Mayer worked with hip hop artist and producer [Kanye West](http://www.last.fm/music/Kanye+West), appearing both on [Go](http://www.last.fm/music/Common/Go) and Kanye West’s Bittersweet (released in the summer of 2007 as an iTunes pre-order bonus track to the album _Graduation_) and received praise from rap heavyweights like [Jay-Z](http://www.last.fm/music/Jay-Z) and [Nelly](http://www.last.fm/music/Nelly). When asked about his ubiquitous presence in the hip hop community, he said, “It’s not music out there right now.
