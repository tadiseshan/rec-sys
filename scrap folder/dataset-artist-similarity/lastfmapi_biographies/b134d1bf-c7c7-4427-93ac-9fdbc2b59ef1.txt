Meat Loaf (born Marvin Lee Aday on September 27, 1947 in Dallas, Texas) is an American actor and rock and roll performer who came to fame with his album _[Bat Out of Hell](http://www.last.fm/music/Meat+Loaf/Bat+Out+of+Hell)_ and for his movie performances, including Eddie in _[The Rocky Horror Picture Show ](http://www.last.fm/music/Richard+O%27Brien/The+Rocky+Horror+Picture+Show)_, Robert “Bob” Paulson in _[Fight Club](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Dust+Brothers/Fight+Club)_ and Jack Black’s father in [Tenacious D](http://www.last.fm/music/Tenacious+D) in _[The Pick of Destiny.](http://www.last.fm/music/Tenacious+D/The+Pick+of+Destiny.)_      Meat Loaf has had a very successful music career spawning some of the greatest selling albums of all time and breaking various records. Although he enjoyed success with [Jim Steinman](http://www.last.fm/music/Jim+Steinman)’s _Bat Out Of Hell_ Trilogy, he experienced some initial difficulty establishing a steady career within the United States; however, he is considered a legend in the UK and across Europe. In the UK, he is ranked 23rd for number of weeks in the charts overall. Due to his anthemic songs and a performance for Radio Veronica in the [Netherlands](http://www.last.fm/place/Netherlands), he stormed up their Top 100 of all times making a top 10 spot 6 years in a row. He ranks at 96th on VH1’s “100 Greatest Artists of [hard rock](http://www.last.fm/tag/hard%20rock).” His overall success in Europe and [Australia](http://www.last.fm/place/Australia) has been exceptional and he is considered one of the top artists of all time.
