Viikate (meaning “Scythe” in English) is a heavy metal rock band, formed in 1996 in Kouvola, Finland. It is known for its melancholy lyrics, drawing inspiration from Finnish romance movies of the 1950s and Finnish singers of the era, including [Reino Helismaa](http://www.last.fm/music/Reino+Helismaa). Their style has been variously described as “Helismaa-metal”, “[wire](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rautalanka)-metal” and “death [schlager](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Schlager)”.      The band began with Kaarle Viikate and Simeoni Viikate who remained the only members of the band until 2005, when Arvo and Ervo, the band’s long-time live members, joined the band officially. The band’s most well-known hits are “Ei ole ketään kelle soittaa” (There’s no one to call), “Leimu” (Flame), and “Pohjoista Viljaa” (Northern Harvest).
