**Keith Noel Emerson** (born 2 November 1944 in Todmorden, England, United Kingdom), is an [english](http://www.last.fm/tag/english) keyboard player and composer. Formerly a member of the Keith Emerson Trio, [John Brown’s Bodies](http://www.last.fm/music/John+Brown%27s+Bodies), [The T-Bones](http://www.last.fm/music/The+T-Bones), [The V.I.P.’s](http://www.last.fm/music/The+V.I.P.%27s), [P.P. Arnold](http://www.last.fm/music/+noredirect/P.P.+Arnold)’s backing band, and [The Nice](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Nice) (which evolved from P.P. Arnold’s band), he was a founder of [Emerson, Lake & Palmer](http://www.last.fm/music/Emerson,+Lake+&+Palmer) (ELP), one of the early supergroups, in 1970. Following the break-up of ELP, circa 1979, Emerson had modest success with [Emerson, Lake & Powell](http://www.last.fm/music/Emerson,+Lake+&+Powell) in the 1980s as well as with [3](http://www.last.fm/music/3), with the album [To The Power Of Three](http://www.last.fm/music/3/To+The+Power+Of+Three). ELP reunited during the early 1990s, releasing the album [Black Moon](http://www.last.fm/music/Emerson,+Lake+&+Palmer/Black+Moon). Emerson also reunited The Nice in 2002 for a tour. His latest album, Keith Emerson Band featuring [Marc Bonilla](http://www.last.fm/music/Marc+Bonilla), was released in 2008.      Along with contemporaries [Richard Wright](http://www.last.fm/music/Richard+Wright) of [Pink Floyd](http://www.last.fm/music/Pink+Floyd), [Tony Banks](http://www.last.fm/music/Tony+Banks) of [Genesis](http://www.last.fm/music/Genesis), [Billy Ritchie](http://www.last.fm/music/Billy+Ritchie) of [Clouds](http://www.last.fm/music/Clouds), [Rick Wakeman](http://www.last.fm/music/Rick+Wakeman) of [Yes](http://www.last.fm/music/Yes), and [Jon Lord](http://www.last.fm/music/Jon+Lord) of [Deep Purple](http://www.last.fm/music/Deep+Purple), Emerson is widely regarded as one of the top keyboard players of the [progressive rock](http://www.last.fm/tag/progressive%20rock) era. Allmusic refers to Emerson as “perhaps the greatest, most technically accomplished keyboardist in rock history”.
