**Gerald Levert** (July 13 1966 - November 10 2006) was an [rhythm and blues](http://www.last.fm/tag/rhythm%20and%20blues) singer, one of several from the musical Levert family. His father, [Eddie Levert](http://www.last.fm/music/Eddie+Levert), is the lead singer of the 1970s soul group [The O’Jays](http://www.last.fm/music/The+O%27Jays). **Gerald Levert** sang with his brother, [Sean Levert](http://www.last.fm/music/Sean+Levert), and [Marc Gordon](http://www.last.fm/music/Marc+Gordon) in the R&B trio [LeVert](http://www.last.fm/music/LeVert). He was also a member of [LSG](http://www.last.fm/music/LSG), an R&B trio also featuring [Johnny Gill](http://www.last.fm/music/Johnny+Gill) and [Keith Sweat](http://www.last.fm/music/Keith+Sweat)      During the 1980s and early 1990s, **Levert** scored big hits with “[Pop, Pop, Pop, Pop (Goes My Mind)](http://www.last.fm/music/Gerald+Levert/_/Pop,+Pop,+Po p,+Pop+\(Goes+My+Mind\))”, “[Casanova](http://www.last.fm/music/Gerald+Levert/_/Casanova)”, and “[ABC-123](http://www.last.fm/music/Gerald+Levert/_/ABC-123)” (not to be confused with the [Jackson Five](http://www.last.fm/music/+noredirect/Jackson+Five) hit of the same name). As a solo artist, Gerald’s hits included “I’d Give Anything (to Fall in Love)”, “[You Got that Love](http://www.last.fm/music/Gerald+Levert/_/You+Got+that+Love)”, and “[Mr. Too Damn Good To You](http://www.last.fm/music/Gerald+Levert/_/Mr.+Too+Damn+Good+To+You)”.      **Gerald** and Eddie collaborated together on many different occasions, and they recorded an album called [Father and Son](http://www.last.fm/music/Gerald+Levert/Father+and+Son) together.      **Gerald Levert** also sang the chorus on the [Chris Rock](http://www.last.fm/music/Chris+Rock) spoken-word comedy piece, “[No Sex (In The Champagne Room)](http://www.last.fm/music/Chris+Rock/_/No+Sex+\(In+The+Champagne+Room\))”.      In 2005, Levert’s daughter Carlysia was featured on an episode of MTV’s My Super Sweet Sixteen.      **Levert** passed away due to an apparent heart attack on November 10, 2006. Sister 2 Sister magazine reported that **Gerald** and his father had just returned from a 10-day trip to South Africa where they had met with Nelson Mandela and were treated like royalty. Upon Gerald’s return, he was excited about his upcoming album, which is due early in 2007. **Gerald** died in his sleep.
