Tony Levin (born June 6, 1946, Boston, Massachusetts) is an American [rock](http://www.last.fm/tag/rock) musician, specializing in bass guitar, Chapman Stick and upright bass (although he also sings, plays tuba and synthesizer).      Levin is best-known for his work with [progressive rock](http://www.last.fm/tag/progressive%20rock) pioneers [King Crimson](http://www.last.fm/music/King+Crimson) and [Peter Gabriel](http://www.last.fm/music/Peter+Gabriel). He has also been a member of [Liquid Tension Experiment](http://www.last.fm/music/Liquid+Tension+Experiment), the King Crimson-related bands [Bruford Levin Upper Extremities](http://www.last.fm/music/Bruford+Levin+Upper+Extremities), [ProjeKct One](http://www.last.fm/music/ProjeKct+One) and [ProjeKct Four](http://www.last.fm/music/ProjeKct+Four); his own [Tony Levin Band](http://www.last.fm/music/Tony+Levin+Band) includes [Larry Fast](http://www.last.fm/music/Larry+Fast) on keyboards, [Jerry Marotta](http://www.last.fm/music/Jerry+Marotta) on drums, and [Jesse Gress](http://www.last.fm/music/Jesse+Gress) on guitar; his 2007 album _Stick Man_ led to the formation of the band [Stick Men](http://www.last.fm/music/Stick+Men) with fellow Crimson alum [Pat Mastelotto](http://www.last.fm/music/Pat+Mastelotto) and fellow Stickist [Michael Bernier](http://www.last.fm/music/Michael+Bernier) (later replaced with touch guitarist [Markus Reuter](http://www.last.fm/music/Markus+Reuter)).      A prolific session musician since the 1970s, Levin has played on five hundred albums, including those of [Cher](http://www.last.fm/music/Cher), [Alice Cooper](http://www.last.fm/music/Alice+Cooper), [John Lennon](http://www.last.fm/music/John+Lennon), [Sarah McLachlan](http://www.last.fm/music/Sarah+McLachlan), [Stevie Nicks](http://www.last.fm/music/Stevie+Nicks), [Pink Floyd](http://www.last.fm/music/Pink+Floyd), [Lou Reed](http://www.last.fm/music/Lou+Reed), [Buddy Rich](http://www.last.fm/music/Buddy+Rich), [The Roches](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Roches), [Todd Rundgren](http://www.last.fm/music/Todd+Rundgren), [Seal](http://www.last.fm/music/Seal), and [Yes](http://www.last.fm/music/Yes).
