Darius Rucker (born May 13, 1966 in Charleston, South Carolina[1]) is the lead singer and rhythm guitarist of the American [rock](http://www.last.fm/tag/rock) band [Hootie & The Blowfish](http://www.last.fm/music/Hootie+&+The+Blowfish), of which he has been a member since the band’s inception in 1989.      Along with his work in [Hootie & The Blowfish](http://www.last.fm/music/Hootie+&+The+Blowfish), Rucker recorded one studio album, entitled Back to Then, on Hidden Beach Recordings in 2002. In 2008, he signed to Capitol Nashville and charted his first solo single, “[Don’t Think I Don’t Think About It](http://www.last.fm/music/Darius+Rucker/_ /Don%27t+Think+I+Don%27t+Think+About+It)”, the first single from his first [country](http://www.last.fm/tag/country) album [Learn To Live](http://www.last.fm/music/Darius+Rucker/Learn+To+Live).      Rucker has been a member of [Hootie & The Blowfish](http://www.last.fm/music/Hootie+&+The+Blowfish) since their original formation in 1989. He met fellow band members, [Mark Bryan](http://www.last.fm/music/Mark+Bryan), Jim “Soni” Sonefeld, and Dean Felber, while attending the University of South Carolina.      In 2001, he made his solo R&B debut album The Return of Mongo Slade for Atlantic Records. Because of contractual changes, it was never released by the label.[1] Hidden Beach Recordings, an independent label, acquired the masters from Atlantic, releasing the album as Back to Then in July 2002.[1]      Rucker also portrayed a singing cowboy in a television commercial for the fast food chain Burger King, promoting their TenderCrisp Bacon Cheddar Ranch sandwich in 2005; in the commercial, he sang a parody of “Big Rock Candy Mountain”.[2]      In early 2008, Rucker signed to Capitol Records Nashville as the beginning of a career in [country](http://www.last.fm/tag/country) music. His first [country](http://www.last.fm/tag/country) single, “[Don’t Think I Don’t Think About It](http://www.last.fm/music/Darius+Rucker/_/Don%27t+Think+I+Don%27t+Thi nk+About+It)” (which he co-wrote with Clay Mills) debuted at #51 on Billboard’s Hot [country](http://www.last.fm/tag/country) Songs charts for the week of May 3, 2008, and it will serve as the lead-off single to an upcoming album for Capitol, [Learn To Live](http://www.last.fm/music/Darius+Rucker/Learn+To+Live).[3]
