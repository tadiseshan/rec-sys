Curtis James Jackson III (born July 6, 1975 in South Jamaica, Queens, New York), better known by his stage name 50 Cent, is a Grammy award nominated rapper, actor, singer, entrepreneur, author and founder of the hip hop group and label [G-Unit](http://www.last.fm/music/G-Unit).      After leaving drug dealing to pursue a rap career, he released his debut album Guess Who’s Back Again in 2002. He was discovered by [Eminem](http://www.last.fm/music/Eminem) and [Dr. Dre](http://www.last.fm/music/Dr.+Dre) and then signed to [Interscope Records](http://www.last.fm/label/Interscope+Records). He has since released [Get Rich or Die Tryin’](http://www.last.fm/music/50%252Bcent/Get+Rich+or+Die+Tryin%27) (2003), [The Massacre](http://www.last.fm/music/50%252Bcent/The+Massacre) (2006), [Curtis](http://www.last.fm/music/50%252Bcent/Curtis) (2007) and [Before I Self Destruct](http://www.last.fm/music/50%252Bcent/Before+I+Self+Destruct) (2009).      50 Cent has engaged in feuds with other rappers including [Jay-Z](http://www.last.fm/music/Jay-Z), [Ja Rule](http://www.last.fm/music/Ja+Rule), [The Game](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Game), [Cam’ron](http://www.last.fm/music/Cam%27ron), [Fat Joe](http://www.last.fm/music/Fat+Joe), and [Rick Ross](http://www.last.fm/music/Rick+Ross). He has also pursued an acting career, appearing in the semi-autobiographical film Get Rich or Die Tryin’ in 2005, the Iraq War film Home of the Brave in 2006, and Righteous Kill in 2008. 50 Cent is one of the richest hip-hop performers, having a net worth estimated at US $440 million in 2008.      Before 50 Cent ever inked a major deal, he built up a substantial fan base in New York through underground mixtapes. 50 Cent met up with Jam Master Jay of Run-DMC and was signed to his label to write all of Jay’s music. After he’d left JMJ, he signed to Columbia Records in 1996. On this label, he released Power of the Dollar, and the singles How to Rob and Ghetto Qua Ran. [Columbia Records](http://www.last.fm/label/Columbia+Records) released him from the label after being shot on May 24th, 2000.      Around this time, [Eminem](http://www.last.fm/music/Eminem) had heard one of 50 Cent’s demo tapes and expressed a huge interest in him on MTV, as did [Dr. Dre](http://www.last.fm/music/Dr.+Dre). Shortly after this, 50 Cent officially signed to [Interscope Records](http://www.last.fm/label/Interscope+Records). He was the first rapper to sign to the joint label owned by [Dr. Dre](http://www.last.fm/music/Dr.+Dre) and [artistEminem. The first song he released on this record was entitled Wanksta and was featured on the 8 Mile soundtrack.      In its first week of release, his debut ”Get Rich Or Die Tryin’” sold 872,000 units - easily reaching Gold status. Within the second week it went Platinum (1 mil) and on April 12th, 2004 the RIAA certified it six times Platinum.
