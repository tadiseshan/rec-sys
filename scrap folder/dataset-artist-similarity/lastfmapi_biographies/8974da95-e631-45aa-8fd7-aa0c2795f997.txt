**Harry Gregson-Williams** (born December 13, 1961) is a Golden Globe and Grammy-nominated British film score composer.      Early in his career, Harry Gregson-Williams held a position in the 1980s as a music teacher to pupils at the Amesbury School in Hindhead, Surrey, England (his brother Rupert, also a film composer, also taught at Amesbury School during this period).      He later taught music at the Guildhall School of Music & Drama, where he had been a pupil, and also for a short period in [Egypt](http://www.last.fm/place/Egypt).      In addition to scoring a number of motion pictures including _[Kingdom of Heaven](http://www.last.fm/music/Harry+Gregson-Williams/Kingdom+of+Heaven)_, _[The Chronicles of Narnia: The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe](http://www.last.fm/music/Harry+Gregson- Williams/The+Chronicles+of+Narnia:+The+Lion,+the+Witch+and+the+Wardrobe)_, _[Sinbad: Legend of the Seven Seas](http://www.last.fm/music/Harry+Gregson- Williams/Sinbad:+Legend+of+the+Seven+Seas)_, _[Shrek](http://www.last.fm/music/Harry+Gregson-Williams/Shrek)_, _[Deja Vu](http://www.last.fm/music/Harry+Gregson-Williams/Deja+Vu)_, _[Chicken Run](http://www.last.fm/music/Harry+Gregson-Williams/Chicken+Run)_, _[Antz](http://www.last.fm/music/Harry+Gregson-Williams/Antz)_, _[Spy Game](http://www.last.fm/music/Harry+Gregson-Williams/Spy+Game)_, _[Man on Fire](http://www.last.fm/music/Harry+Gregson-Williams/Man+on+Fire)_, and _[Team America: World Police](http://www.last.fm/music/Harry+Gregson- Williams/Team+America:+World+Police)_, Gregson-Williams is also noted for his work on the popular video games _Metal Gear Solid 2: Sons of Liberty_, _Metal Gear Solid 3: Snake Eater_, _Metal Gear Solid 4: Guns of the Patriots_, and _ Call of Duty 4: Modern Warfare_. His arrangement of the original _Metal Gear Solid_ Main Theme was just the first of many striking works he created for the _Metal Gear Solid_ productions that involved both orchestral and electronic textures. His soundtrack for _Metal Gear Solid 2: Sons of Liberty_ was generally considered a resounding success and an important step forward in blending the mediums of film and video game.      In his score to the Ridley Scott film _[Kingdom of Heaven](http://www.last.fm/music/Harry+Gregson-Williams/Kingdom+of+Heaven)_, he introduced a mix of operatic and Middle Eastern themes. One track, “[Ibelin](http://www.last.fm/music/Harry+Gregson-Williams/_/Ibelin),” was reprised as the closing credits theme with singer [Natacha Atlas](http://www.last.fm/music/Natacha+Atlas) performing Arabic lyrics.      _[The Chronicles of Narnia: The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe](http://www.last.fm/music/Harry+Gregson- Williams/The+Chronicles+of+Narnia:+The+Lion,+the+Witch+and+the+Wardrobe)_ was released on December 9th, 2005. He was contracted to score the next in the series, _[The Chronicles Of Narnia: Prince Caspian](http://www.last.fm/music/Harry+Gregson- Williams/The+Chronicles+Of+Narnia:+Prince+Caspian)_
