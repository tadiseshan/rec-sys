Denison Witmer (born November 4, 1976, in Lancaster, Pennsylvania) is an American indie singer-songwriter. His first release was a cassette in 1995 entitled “[My Luck, My Love](http://www.last.fm/music/Denison+Witmer/My+Luck,+My+Love)”.      Witmer has released a number of albums (including [Recovered](http://www.last.fm/music/Denison+Witmer/Recovered)), some EPs, and an LP with his side project, [The River Bends](http://www.last.fm/music/The+River+Bends), in which Witmer plays guitar and sings along with a backup band including members of Philly-based alt- country band [One Star Hotel](http://www.last.fm/music/One+Star+Hotel). His 2005 album, [Are You a Dreamer?](http://www.last.fm/music/Denison+Witmer/Are+You+a+Dreamer%3F), was produced by mentor [Don Peris](http://www.last.fm/music/Don+Peris) of [The Innocence Mission](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Innocence+Mission) and featured [Asthmatic Kitty](http://www.asthmatickitty.com) artist [Sufjan Stevens](http://www.last.fm/music/Sufjan+Stevens). It gained rave reviews by such opposite ends of the spectrum as [Pitchfork Media](http://www.pitchforkmedia.com) and [Entertainment Weekly](http://www.ew.com).      Subsequent albums have included “Carry the Weight” (2008), “The Ones Who Wait” (2012), and “Denison Witmer” (2013).      His official website is located at <http://www.denisonwitmer.com>.
