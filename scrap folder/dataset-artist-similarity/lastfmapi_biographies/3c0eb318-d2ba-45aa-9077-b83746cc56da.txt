Amanda MacKinnon Gaiman Palmer is an American vocalist, pianist and performance artist, best known for her work as one half of [The Dresden Dolls](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Dresden+Dolls). Born in 1976, she grew up in Lexington, Massachusetts and received her B.A. from Wesleyan University. She attended Lexington High School, where she was very involved in the drama department. Amanda sometimes has Lexington High School students perform drama pieces at her shows. Before she was in the Dresden Dolls, she was in a band called “Amanda Palmer and the Void”. In October of 2000, she met [Brian Viglione](http://www.last.fm/music/Brian+Viglione), a drummer; together they formed the Dresden Dolls.      In the 2005, WNFX/Boston Phoenix Best Music Poll Amanda Palmer won Best Female Vocalist.      Amanda has performed as a living statue called “The Eight Foot Bride” in Harvard Square as well as in many other locations.      Her solo album entitled “[Who Killed Amanda Palmer](http://www.last.fm/music/Amanda+Palmer/Who+Killed+Amanda+Palmer)” was released in September 2008, and was produced by [Ben Folds](http://www.last.fm/music/Ben+Folds), who also appears on the album. [Zoë Keating](http://www.last.fm/music/Zo%C3%AB+Keating) also appears on several tracks.      In 2009, she pleaded with her label, [Roadrunner Records](http://www.last.fm/label/Roadrunner+Records), to drop her from their roster. She has been vocal about this in interviews and in concert, even dedicating a song called “[Please Drop Me](http://www.last.fm/music/Amanda+Palmer/_/Please+Drop+Me)” that is sung to the tune of “Moon River.”      On January 15, 2010, writer [Neil Gaiman](http://www.last.fm/music/Neil+Gaiman) announced on his official blog that he and Palmer were engaged to be married.      On March 30, 2010, the album “[Evelyn Evelyn](http://www.last.fm/music/Evelyn+Evelyn/Evelyn+Evelyn)” was released, as a collaborative work with [Jason Webley](http://www.last.fm/music/Jason+Webley).      On July 20, 2010, Palmer released a solo EP of [Radiohead](http://www.last.fm/music/Radiohead) covers, entitled “[Amanda Palmer Performs the Popular Hits of Radiohead on Her Magical Ukulele](http://w ww.last.fm/music/Amanda+Palmer/Amanda+Palmer+Performs+the+Popular+Hits+of+Radi ohead+on+Her+Magical+Ukulele)”.      In January of 2011, Palmer and Gaiman announced that they were now legally married. Palmer also released a new studio album, _[Amanda Palmer Goes Down Under](http://www.last.fm/music/Amanda+Palmer/Amanda+Palmer+Goes+Down+Under)_.      On January 21, 2011, Palmer released “[Amanda Palmer Goes Down Under](http://www.last.fm/music/Amanda+Palmer/Amanda+Palmer+Goes+Down+Under), an album with an Antipodean theme featuring songs that Palmer wrote about, or while in, Australia and New Zealand.
