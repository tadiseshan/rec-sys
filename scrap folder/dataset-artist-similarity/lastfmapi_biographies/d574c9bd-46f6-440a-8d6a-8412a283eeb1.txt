**William James Adams Jr.** (born March 15, 1975), better known as **will.i.am**, is an American [hip-hop](http://www.last.fm/tag/hip-hop) and [pop](http://www.last.fm/tag/pop) musician and founding member of the [Black Eyed Peas](http://www.last.fm/music/Black+Eyed+Peas). Adams is of Jamaican descent and has rapped since his teenage years. Along with co-writing many of the Peas’ hit songs, he’s also had a solo career featuring multiple top 20 hit singles as well as collaborations with various artists such as [Mick Jagger](http://www.last.fm/music/Mick+Jagger) and [Britney Spears](http://www.last.fm/music/Britney+Spears) among many others.      When William was sixteen years old, he started rapping in late 1991 under the name “Will-1X” after being discovered by Compton rapper and former N.W.A. leader [Eazy-E](http://www.last.fm/music/Eazy-E) and signed a deal to Eazy’s Ruthless Records. will.i.am’s first rap song was “Merry Muthafuckin’ Xmas” from Eazy-E’s 1992 EP _5150: Home 4 tha Sick_. Shortly after Eazy-E’s death in 1995, Will left Ruthless to start a group that would later be called the Black Eyed Peas with fellow rappers [Taboo](http://www.last.fm/music/Taboo) and [apl.de.ap](http://www.last.fm/music/apl.de.ap).      After the addition of [singer-songwriter](http://www.last.fm/tag/singer- songwriter) [Fergie](http://www.last.fm/music/Fergie), the group shot into mainstream pop consciousness with a set of hit singles in the mid-00s, popularity that continues to this day. The Black Eyed Peas’ producer is Sergio Mendes, an internationally successful Brazilian musician and jazz aficionado. In September 2007, will.i.am took a major step into a solo career identity by joining with his record label, Universal Music Group (UMG), on a pilot scheme to reward certain file-sharing with cash incentives, including will.i.am tracks from his then upcoming release _[Songs About Girls](http://www.last.fm/music/will.i.am/Songs+About+Girls)_.      In total, he has released four solo albums: _[Lost Change](http://www.last.fm/music/will.i.am/Lost+Change)_ (2001), _[Must B 21](http://www.last.fm/music/will.i.am/Must+B+21)_ (2003), _[Songs About Girls](http://www.last.fm/music/will.i.am/Songs+About+Girls)_ (2007), and _[#willpower](http://www.last.fm/music/will.i.am/%23willpower)_ (2013). Although often facing mixed critical reviews, he’s experienced significant commercial success, including a set of popular singles. His solo performances, featuring collaborations with a wide group of artists including [Justin Bieber](http://www.last.fm/music/Justin+Bieber), [Chris Brown](http://www.last.fm/music/Chris+Brown), [Phife Dawg](http://www.last.fm/music/Phife+Dawg), [Snoop Dogg](http://www.last.fm/music/Snoop+Dogg), and [Nicole Scherzinger](http://www.last.fm/music/Nicole+Scherzinger), are perhaps best summed up in the international top 20 mega-hit “[Scream & Shout](http://www.last.fm/music/will.i.am/_/Scream+&+Shout)”.
