**Ryan Adams** (born David Ryan Adams on November 5, 1974) is an [alt-country](http://www.last.fm/tag/alt-country) and [rock and roll](http://www.last.fm/tag/rock%20and%20roll) [singer-songwriter](http://www.last.fm/tag/singer-songwriter) from Jacksonville, North Carolina. Initially part of the group [Whiskeytown](http://www.last.fm/music/Whiskeytown), which was at the time considered one of the leaders of the alt-country genre, Adams left the band and released his first solo album, Heartbreaker, in 2000 (which was subsequently nominated for the Shortlist Music Prize). As well as his solo albums, Adams has released five albums as [Ryan Adams & The Cardinals](http://www.last.fm/music/Ryan+Adams+&+The+Cardinals).      Adams dropped out of high school at age 16 to work in a shoe shop and make music. He formed a band named [Whiskeytown](http://www.last.fm/music/Whiskeytown) in 1994; they disbanded in 1999 having released two full albums, _[Faithless Street](http://www.last.fm/music/Whiskeytown/Faithless+Street)_ and _[Strangers Almanac](http://www.last.fm/music/Whiskeytown/Strangers+Almanac)_. Adams went on to put out his first solo record, _[Heartbreaker](http://www.last.fm/music/Ryan+Adams/Heartbreaker)_, in 2000. In 2002, Ryan contributed backing vocals on [Butterfly In Reverse](http://www.last.fm/music/Counting+Crows/_/Butterfly+In+Reverse), from the [Counting Crows](http://www.last.fm/music/Counting+Crows) album, Hard Candy; after [Adam Duritz](http://www.last.fm/music/Adam+Duritz) (Counting Crows lead singer) appeared on the album _[Gold](http://www.last.fm/music/Ryan+Adams/Gold)_.      Ryan is highly prolific, and in 2005 released one double album and two regular albums: _[Cold Roses](http://www.last.fm/music/Ryan%2BAdams%2B%2526%2BThe%2BCa rdinals/Cold+Roses)_, _[Jacksonville City Nights](http://www.last.fm/music/Rya n%2BAdams%2B%2526%2BThe%2BCardinals/Jacksonville+City+Nights)_ and _[29](http://www.last.fm/music/Ryan+Adams/29)_, respectively. His album, _29_, was produced by friend [Ethan Johns](http://www.last.fm/music/Ethan+Johns) (who also produced his first two albums, _[Heartbreaker](http://www.last.fm/music/Ryan+Adams/Heartbreaker)_ and _[Gold](http://www.last.fm/music/Ryan+Adams/Gold)_). In between those two releases and his 2005 triple-header, Ryan released _[Demolition](http://www.last.fm/music/Ryan+Adams/Demolition)_ (a collection of out-takes), _[Love Is Hell](http://www.last.fm/music/Ryan+Adams/Love+Is+Hell)_ and _[Rock N Roll](http://www.last.fm/music/Ryan+Adams/Rock+N+Roll)_. Unreleased studio albums include _[48 Hours](http://www.last.fm/music/Ryan+Adams/48+Hours)_, _[Exile On Franklin Street](http://www.last.fm/music/Ryan+Adams/Exile+On+Franklin+Street)_ and _[Suicide Handbook](http://www.last.fm/music/Ryan+Adams/Suicide+Handbook)_
