**George Duke** (b. 12 Jan 1946 in San Rafael, CA, USA - d. 5 Aug 2013 in Los Angeles, CA), was a pianist and synthesizer pioneer, who also majored in the trombone. Early influences: **[Les McCann](http://www.last.fm/music/Les+McCann)** and cousin **Charles Burrell**. First gig was with the house band at the Half Note club and with vocal band [Third Wave](http://www.last.fm/music/Third+Wave) in 1968, from there he backed such musicians as **[Dizzy Gillespie](http://www.last.fm/music/Dizzy+Gillespie)**, **[Kenny Dorham](http://www.last.fm/music/Kenny+Dorham)** and **[Don Ellis](http://www.last.fm/music/Don+Ellis)**, playing session musician on Jean-Luc Pontys Pacific Jazz Albums.1970 was a milestone for **Duke** playing on The Mothers of Invention album 200 Motels.      He then went on to join [The Cannonball Adderley Quintet](http://www.last.fm/music/+noredirect/The+Cannonball+Adderley+Quintet) recording at least six albums with **[Adderley](music/Cannonball+Adderley)**. Further collaborations with **[Frank Zappa](http://www.last.fm/music/Frank+Zappa)** and **[Billy Cobham](http://www.last.fm/music/Billy+Cobham)** came his first venture into the charts with “Reach for It” and “Dukey Stick”. Reverting to his [jazz](http://www.last.fm/tag/jazz) roots his “**[Brazilian Love Affair](http://www.last.fm/music/George+Duke/Brazilian+Love+Affair)**” album even impressed his jazz critics after many accused him of selling out to [pop](http://www.last.fm/tag/pop).
