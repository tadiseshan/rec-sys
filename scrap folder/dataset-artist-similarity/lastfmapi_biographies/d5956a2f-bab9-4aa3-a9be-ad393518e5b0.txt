Dominguinhos (lit. “Little Dominic” - born José Domingos de Morais in Garanhuns) is a Brazilian and one of the greatest [accordion](http://www.last.fm/tag/accordion) players of [forro](http://www.last.fm/tag/forro) (i.e., forró) music. Dominguinhos was musically adopted by the great [Luiz Gonzaga](http://www.last.fm/music/Luiz+Gonzaga), the king of Baião ([brazilian](http://www.last.fm/tag/brazilian) [nordeste](http://www.last.fm/tag/nordeste)—northeast music). Dominguinhos was an accomplished musician, singer and composer, faithful to the roots of forró, with a jazzistic approach to his playing.      He has performed with musicians such as [Luiz Gonzaga](http://www.last.fm/music/Luiz+Gonzaga), [Caetano Veloso](http://www.last.fm/music/Caetano+Veloso), [Gilberto Gil](http://www.last.fm/music/Gilberto+Gil), [Gal Costa](http://www.last.fm/music/Gal+Costa) (with whom he toured in Midem), [Zé Ramalho](http://www.last.fm/music/Z%C3%A9+Ramalho), [Toquinho](http://www.last.fm/music/Toquinho), and [Maria Bethânia](http://www.last.fm/music/Maria+Beth%C3%A2nia). Some of his hits were recorded by Bethânia, Gil, [Chico Buarque](http://www.last.fm/music/Chico+Buarque), [Elba Ramalho](http://www.last.fm/music/Elba+Ramalho), and [Fagner](http://www.last.fm/music/Fagner). In 1997 Dominguinhos wrote the soundtrack of the film O Cangaceiro.
