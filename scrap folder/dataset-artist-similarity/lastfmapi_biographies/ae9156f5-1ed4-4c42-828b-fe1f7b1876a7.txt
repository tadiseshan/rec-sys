**Buddy Miller** is a country singer, songwriter, recording artist and producer, currently living in Nashville, Tennessee. Miller is married to and has recorded with singer/songwriter [Julie Miller](http://www.last.fm/music/Julie+Miller).      Miller formed the Buddy Miller Band, which included singer/songwriter [Shawn Colvin](http://www.last.fm/music/Shawn+Colvin) on vocals and guitar.      In addition to releasing several solo albums over the years, Miller has toured as lead guitarist/backing vocalist for [Emmylou Harris](http://www.last.fm/music/Emmylou+Harris)’s Spyboy band, [Steve Earle](http://www.last.fm/music/Steve+Earle) on his El Corazon tour, and [Linda Ronstadt](http://www.last.fm/music/Linda+Ronstadt). He co-produced and performed on [Jimmie Dale Gilmore](http://www.last.fm/music/Jimmie+Dale+Gilmore)’s 2000 album Endless Night. He has also appeared on several albums by songwriter/singer [Lucinda Williams](http://www.last.fm/music/Lucinda+Williams).      At the 4th Annual Americana Music Association Honors & Awards, the Ryman Auditorium September 2005, Miller received the Album of the Year Award for his [Universal United House Of Prayer](http://www.last.fm/music/Buddy+Miller/Unive rsal+United+House+Of+Prayer), and the opening cut of that album, [Worry Too Much](http://www.last.fm/music/Buddy+Miller/_/Worry+Too+Much) (penned by [Mark Heard](http://www.last.fm/music/Mark+Heard), and originally released on [Second Hand](http://www.last.fm/music/Mark+Heard/Second+Hand)), won the Song of the Year Award.
