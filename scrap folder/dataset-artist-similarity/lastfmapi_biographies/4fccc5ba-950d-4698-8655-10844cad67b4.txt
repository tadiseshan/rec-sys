Natalie MacMaster (born June 13, 1972) is an award-winning fiddler from the rural community of Troy in Inverness County, Nova Scotia, Canada. She is one of the best-known musicians in the tradition of the driving [cape breton](http://www.last.fm/tag/cape%20breton) fiddle music style, and has made a highly successful career as a touring musician, traveling to Europe and Asia and doing as many as 250 shows in a year. From a large musical family, she is the niece of Cape Breton fiddler [Buddy MacMaster](http://www.last.fm/music/Buddy+MacMaster) and the cousin of musicians [Ashley MacIsaac](http://www.last.fm/music/Ashley+MacIsaac) and [Andrea Beaton](http://www.last.fm/music/Andrea+Beaton).      MacMaster has toured with many famous performers, including the [Chieftains](http://www.last.fm/music/+noredirect/Chieftains), [Faith Hill](http://www.last.fm/music/Faith+Hill), [Carlos Santana](http://www.last.fm/music/Carlos+Santana) and [Alison Krauss](http://www.last.fm/music/Alison+Krauss). She has appeared at many music festivals worldwide, including the Celtic Colours festival in Cape Breton, Celtic Connections in Scotland, and MerleFest in the United States.
