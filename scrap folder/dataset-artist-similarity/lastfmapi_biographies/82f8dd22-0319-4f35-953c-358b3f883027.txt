**Angie Stone** (born Angela Laverne Brown in January 30, 1961, in Columbia, South Carolina) is a Grammy Award-nominated American R&B, hip-hop, and neo-soul singer and actress. Stone’s music possesses a clear old school soul music influence, and her singing vocals recall those of [Aretha Franklin](http://www.last.fm/music/Aretha+Franklin).         In the early 1980s, **Stone** (as ‘Angie B’), was a member of [The Sequence](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Sequence), the first major female hip- hop group. They had a hit in 1980 with “[Funk You Up](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Sequence/_/Funk+You+Up)”. She then worked with [Mantronix](http://www.last.fm/music/Mantronix), before teaming up and performing vocals and saxophone for [Lenny Kravitz](http://www.last.fm/music/Lenny+Kravitz).         **Angie Stone** emerged during the 1990’s as part of hip-hop/R&B trio [Vertical Hold](http://www.last.fm/music/Vertical+Hold) which released the popular single “[Seems You’re Much Too Busy](http://www.last.fm/music/Vertical+Hold/_/Seems+You%27re+Much+Too+Busy)” as well as two albums: [A Matter of Time](http://www.last.fm/music/Vertical+Hold/A+Matter+of+Time) (June 1993) and [Head First](http://www.last.fm/music/Vertical+Hold/Head+First) (1995).      In 1996, she teamed up with [Gerry DeVeaux](http://www.last.fm/music/Gerry+DeVeaux) ([Lenny Kravitz](http://www.last.fm/music/Lenny+Kravitz)’s cousin from The Bahamas) and together with [Charlie Mole](http://www.last.fm/music/Charlie+Mole) they formed [Devox](http://www.last.fm/music/Devox). One album received a Japanese- only release including Stone-penned or co-written material.      **Angie Stone** shared song-writing credits on [D’Angelo](http://www.last.fm/music/D%27Angelo)’s first two studio album’s (1995 - [Brown Sugar](http://www.last.fm/music/D%27Angelo/Brown+Sugar) and 2000 - [Voodoo](http://www.last.fm/music/D%27Angelo/Voodoo)) as well as providing backing vocals on tour with him.      She released her first solo album, [Black Diamond](http://www.last.fm/music/Angie+Stone/Black+Diamond), released on 28 September 1999 via [Arista](http://www.last.fm/label/Arista) Records. She has since also released, via [Clive Davis](http://www.last.fm/music/Clive+Davis)’ [J Records](http://www.last.fm/label/J+Records), [Mahogany Soul](http://www.last.fm/music/Angie+Stone/Mahogany+Soul) on October 16, 2001 and [Stone Love](http://www.last.fm/music/Angie+Stone/Stone+Love) on July 6, 2004.
