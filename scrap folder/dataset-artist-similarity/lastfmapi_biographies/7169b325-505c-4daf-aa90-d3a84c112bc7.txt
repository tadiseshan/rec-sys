Luiz Gonzaga do Nascimento Júnior (September 22, 1945 — April 29, 1991), better known as Gonzaguinha, was a noted [brazilian](http://www.last.fm/tag/brazilian) singer and composer. He was born in Rio de Janeiro, the son of [Luiz Gonzaga](http://www.last.fm/music/Luiz+Gonzaga) (“Gonzagão”), who was known as the “king of [baiao](http://www.last.fm/tag/baiao)”. Gonzaguinha was killed in a car accident on April 29, 1991, in Curitiba.      Many eminent Brazilian artists recorded his compositions, including [Maria Bethânia](http://www.last.fm/music/Maria+Beth%C3%A2nia), [Simone](http://www.last.fm/music/Simone), [Elis Regina](http://www.last.fm/music/Elis+Regina), [Fagner](http://www.last.fm/music/Fagner), and [Joanna](http://www.last.fm/music/Joanna). Notable covers include hits like “Sangrando”, “Mulher, e daí” and “Começaria tudo outra vez”.
