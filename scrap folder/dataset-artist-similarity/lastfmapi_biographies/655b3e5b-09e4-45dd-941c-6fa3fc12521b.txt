**Matthew Stephen Ward** (b 4th October 1973, Newbury Park, California), known by his stage name **M. Ward**, is a U.S. singer-songwriter and guitarist who rose to prominence in the Portland, Oregon music scene. In addition to his solo work, he is known as a member of [She & Him](http://www.last.fm/music/She+&+Him) and [Monsters of Folk](http://www.last.fm/music/Monsters+of+Folk).      Ward was previously with the band [Rodriguez](http://www.last.fm/music/Rodriguez), which included bassist Kyle Field (of [Little Wings](http://www.last.fm/music/Little+Wings) fame) and drummer Mike Funk. They recorded and released on cassette _Box Plots and Cash Crops_ and then the album _[Swing Like a Metronome](http://www.last.fm/music/Rodriguez/Swing+Like+a+Metronome)_ in 2000. _[Duet for Guitars #2](http://www.last.fm/music/M.+Ward/Duet+for+Guitars+%232)_ was released on [Howe Gelb](http://www.last.fm/music/Howe+Gelb)’s Ow Om record label. Ward’s 2001 album, _[End of Amnesia](http://www.last.fm/music/M.+Ward/End+of+Amnesia)_, was released by Future Farmer Records, and his subsequent albums have been released on Merge Records. A collection of live recordings, _[Live Music & The Voices Of Strange rs](http://www.last.fm/music/M.+Ward/Live+Music+&+The+Voices+Of+Strangers)_, was a self-released disc that was sold at his shows. His next album, _[Transfiguration of Vincent](http://www.last.fm/music/M.+Ward/Transfiguration+of+Vincent)_, was released in 2003, followed by _[Transistor Radio](http://www.last.fm/music/M.+Ward/Transistor+Radio)_ in 2005. A year later, _[Post War](http://www.last.fm/music/M.+Ward/Post+War)_ was released.      In 2006, he contributed a song to, and helped produce, the [John Fahey](http://www.last.fm/music/John+Fahey) tribute album, _I Am the Resurrection_. He also contributed the song “[Transfiguration #1](http://www.last.fm/music/M.+Ward/_/Transfiguration+%231)” from _Transfiguration of Vincent_ to the Brushfire Records soundtrack for _A Brokedown Melody_, a [Jack Johnson](http://www.last.fm/music/Jack+Johnson) surfing film.      His album, _[Hold Time](http://www.last.fm/music/M.+Ward/Hold+Time)_, was released in 2009. The album _[A Wasteland Companion](http://www.last.fm/music/M.+Ward/A+Wasteland+Companion)_ followed in 2012. The album title alludes to T.S. Eliot’s 1922 modernist poem, The Waste Land.      **Side Projects**      In 2008 M. Ward combined forces with [Zooey Deschanel](http://www.last.fm/music/Zooey+Deschanel) and became [She & Him](http://www.last.fm/music/She+&+Him).
