Van Dyke Parks (born January 3, 1943 in Hattiesburg, Mississippi, [United States](http://www.last.fm/place/United+States)) is an American composer, arranger, producer, instrumentalist, singer-songwriter, author, and actor based in Pasadena, California. Parks is perhaps best known for his collaborations with musician [Brian Wilson](http://www.last.fm/music/Brian+Wilson), and especially for his lyrical contributions to [The Beach Boys](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Beach+Boys)’ legendary [Smile](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Beach+Boys/Smile) album.      Among the hundreds of artists he’s worked with include [Phil Ochs](http://www.last.fm/music/Phil+Ochs), [Tim Buckley](http://www.last.fm/music/Tim+Buckley), [Haruomi Hosono](http://www.last.fm/music/+noredirect/Haruomi+Hosono), [The Byrds](http://www.last.fm/music/The+Byrds), [Rufus Wainwright](http://www.last.fm/music/Rufus+Wainwright), [Harry Nilsson](http://www.last.fm/music/Harry+Nilsson), [Randy Newman](http://www.last.fm/music/Randy+Newman), [Ry Cooder](http://www.last.fm/music/Ry+Cooder), [U2](http://www.last.fm/music/U2), [Joanna Newsom](http://www.last.fm/music/Joanna+Newsom), [Inara George](http://www.last.fm/music/Inara+George), [Keith Moon](http://www.last.fm/music/Keith+Moon), [Frank Zappa](http://www.last.fm/music/Frank+Zappa), [Harpers Bizarre](http://www.last.fm/music/Harpers+Bizarre), [Little Feat](http://www.last.fm/music/Little+Feat), and [Ringo Starr](http://www.last.fm/music/Ringo+Starr).      In addition to producing, Parks has released seven studio albums of his own recordings: [Song Cycle](http://www.last.fm/music/Van+Dyke+Parks/Song+Cycle), [Discover America](http://www.last.fm/music/Van+Dyke+Parks/Discover+America), [Clang of the Yankee Reaper](http://www.last.fm/music/Van+Dyke+Parks/Clang+of+the+Yankee+Reaper), [Jump!](http://www.last.fm/music/Van+Dyke+Parks/Jump!), [Tokyo Rose](http://www.last.fm/music/Van+Dyke+Parks/Tokyo+Rose), [Songs Cycled](http://www.last.fm/music/Van+Dyke+Parks/Songs+Cycled), and with Brian Wilson, [Orange Crate Art](http://www.last.fm/music/+noredirect/Brian+Wilson+& +Van+Dyke+Parks/Orange+Crate+Art). He has also released a live album, [Moonlighting: Live at the Ash Grove](http://www.last.fm/music/Van+Dyke+Parks/ Moonlighting:+Live+at+the+Ash+Grove). His first official release was the 1966 single [Number Nine](http://www.last.fm/music/Van+Dyke+Parks/_/Number+Nine), a modernized version of Beethoven’s 9th backed with [Do What You Wanta](http://www.last.fm/music/Van+Dyke+Parks/_/Do+What+You+Wanta).      Parks has also established himself in film scores, and over the years has directed, arranged, produced, and composed soundtracks for a great number of motion pictures and television shows.      During his childhood, Parks became extremely fond of old-style American music, most notably the sounds of [tin pan alley](http://www.last.fm/tag/tin%20pan%20alley)
