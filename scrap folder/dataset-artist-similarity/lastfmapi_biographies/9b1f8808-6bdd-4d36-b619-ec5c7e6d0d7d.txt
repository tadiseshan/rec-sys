Leon Ware (born February 6, 1940 in Detroit, Michigan) is a soul music singer, songwriter and producer. During the 1970s and 1980 he released six albums with moderate success. To date, Ware has ten full-length solo albums to his credit, according to his website [www.leonware.com](http://www.leonware.com)      Ware, disputably, found his biggest success crafting the hit album, [I Want You](http://www.last.fm/music/Marvin+Gaye/I+Want+You), for his friend and [Motown](http://www.last.fm/label/Motown) icon Marvin Gaye in 1976. He is also is notable for writing the Top 5 R&B single, “I Wanna Be Where You Are”, for a young [Michael Jackson](http://www.last.fm/music/Michael+Jackson) in 1972, collaborating with Quincy Jones on the [Body Heat](http://www.last.fm/music/Quincy+Jones/Body+Heat) album, co-writing the R&B hit “Inside My Love” with Minnie Riperton, and working with neo-soul artist Maxwell in the mid-’90s.
