Miles Davis (**Miles Dewey Davis III**, Alton, Illinois, May 26, 1926 – Santa Monica, California, September 28, 1991) was an American [trumpeter](http://www.last.fm/tag/trumpeter), [bandleader](http://www.last.fm/tag/bandleader) and [composer](http://www.last.fm/tag/composer).      Widely considered one of the most influential musicians of the 20th century, [Miles Davis](http://www.milesdavis.com/) was, with his musical groups, at the forefront of several major developments in [jazz](http://www.last.fm/tag/jazz) music, including [bebop](http://www.last.fm/tag/bebop), [cool jazz](http://www.last.fm/tag/cool%20jazz), [hard bop](http://www.last.fm/tag/hard%20bop), [modal jazz](http://www.last.fm/tag/modal%20jazz), and [jazz fusion](http://www.last.fm/tag/jazz%20fusion). Many well-known musicians rose to prominence as members of Davis’ ensembles, including saxophonists [Gerry Mulligan](http://www.last.fm/music/Gerry+Mulligan), [John Coltrane](http://www.last.fm/music/John+Coltrane), [Cannonball Adderley](http://www.last.fm/music/Cannonball+Adderley), [George Coleman](http://www.last.fm/music/George+Coleman), [Wayne Shorter](http://www.last.fm/music/Wayne+Shorter), [Dave Liebman](http://www.last.fm/music/Dave+Liebman), [Branford Marsalis](http://www.last.fm/music/Branford+Marsalis) and [Kenny Garrett](http://www.last.fm/music/Kenny+Garrett); trombonist [J. J. Johnson](http://www.last.fm/music/+noredirect/J.+J.+Johnson); pianists [Horace Silver](http://www.last.fm/music/Horace+Silver), [Red Garland](http://www.last.fm/music/Red+Garland), [Wynton Kelly](http://www.last.fm/music/Wynton+Kelly), [Bill Evans](http://www.last.fm/music/Bill+Evans), [Herbie Hancock](http://www.last.fm/music/Herbie+Hancock), [Joe Zawinul](http://www.last.fm/music/Joe+Zawinul), [Chick Corea](http://www.last.fm/music/Chick+Corea), and [Keith Jarrett](http://www.last.fm/music/Keith+Jarrett); guitarists [John McLaughlin](http://www.last.fm/music/John+McLaughlin), [John Scofield](http://www.last.fm/music/John+Scofield) and [Mike Stern](http://www.last.fm/music/Mike+Stern); bassists [Paul Chambers](http://www.last.fm/music/Paul+Chambers), [Ron Carter](http://www.last.fm/music/Ron+Carter), [Dave Holland](http://www.last.fm/music/Dave+Holland), [Marcus Miller](http://www.last.fm/music/Marcus+Miller) and [Darryl Jones](http://www.last.fm/music/Darryl+Jones), ; and drummers [Philly Joe Jones](http://www.last.fm/music/Philly+Joe+Jones), [Tony Williams](http://www.last.fm/music/Tony+Williams), [Billy Cobham](http://www.last.fm/music/Billy+Cobham), [Jack DeJohnette](http://www.last.fm/music/Jack+DeJohnette), and [Al Foster](http://www.last.fm/music/Al+Foster).      Miles Davis was born on May 26, 1926, to a relatively affluent family in Alton, Illinois. His father, Dr. Miles Henry Davis, was a dentist. In 1927 the family moved to East St. Louis, Illinois. They also owned a substantial ranch in northern Arkansas, where Davis learned to ride horses as a boy
